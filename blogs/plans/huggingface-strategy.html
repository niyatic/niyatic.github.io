1. Why Hugging Face, and why honesty wins here

Hugging Face is the surface where the document-AI research audience actually

lives — practitioners who pull a checkpoint, read the card, and re-run the

numbers. That audience punishes hype and rewards two things: **reproducibility

discipline and honest disclosure**. SAVIOR-Bench is unusually well-suited

to that bar, because its two most "awkward" facts are, on HF, *credibility

assets*:

  • The W6 0/23 finding. A pinned factory re-run was attempted against all

23 parse-intent rows and re-derived **0/23 green — 23/23 red on structural

impossibility, with 0 CIs added, 0 attested rows retracted, 0 silent

flips of reproduced_by_factory**. The structural reasons (no vendor

adapters in the offline SEED harness; the in-house parse-intent corpus is

not in-repo; the attested source carries only aggregate F1/WER, so no

bootstrap CI can be derived) are logged verbatim. Most launches would hide

this. We lead a section with it — *this is what a benchmark that refuses to

fabricate looks like.*

  • The narrow fine-tune edge. The Hyperbots-IP fine-tunes win, but

*narrowly and metric-specifically*: parse-intent F1 +0.0276 over the

best well-documented zero-shot system (GPT-4o 0.8052; the fine-tune is

0.8328), OCR recall +0.0061 over the best zero-shot OCR system. On OCR

precision and layout/PaIRS, zero-shot systems *win* (precision

−0.0165; PaIRS −0.1700). A launch that claimed a blowout would be caught in

a day. A launch that says "fine-tuning buys a real but modest, recall/F1-

specific edge — here is the exact number, and here are the two metrics

where it loses" earns the kind of trust that compounds.

The strategy below makes those two facts the spine of every HF artifact.

3. Dataset-card plan — SAVIOR-Bench v1

Card sections, in order:

  • Dataset summary — 509 expert-annotated financial documents; IAA

0.761 (fuzzy string similarity, 187 double-annotated pairs); five

corpora (UCSF Industry Documents 46.95%, in-house synthetic 17.88%, misc.

  • Tasks & metrics — OCR (word-level multiset recall/precision + PaIRS,

PC-only) and parse-intent (F1 ↑ / WER ↓). Frozen decoding: temp 0.1,

top-p 0.9, max_tokens 2048, seed 20260516.

  • Honesty & provenance block — non-negotiable, placed high. Attested

2026-05-18, not factory-reproduced; ci95=null shown as "reported

(no CI)"; parse-intent n not in the attested source — reported unknown,

not inferred; the W6 0/23 audit summarized with a link to paper §5.1.1.

  • Limitations + the gap roadmap — the §3 related-work matrix marks ✗ on

every dimension SAVIOR-Bench does not yet satisfy (no per-category slices,

no degradation tracks, no CIs, not regenerable); §10 names the R1–R6 work

to close them. The dataset card *shows the ✗ matrix* — that honesty is the

card's differentiator.

  • Access — benchmark access on request, niyati.chhaya@hyprbots.com;

raw labels not redistributed (mixed public-corpus licensing).

4. Leaderboard / Space positioning

  • Positioning, not a claim of category dominance. SAVIOR-Bench is

positioned as a provenance-tagged financial-document benchmark — its

distinctness is financial-document specificity + provenance tagging +

parse-intent coverage, *not* evidence depth. The §3 matrix is shown so the

reader sees exactly where it leads and where it trails.

  • The leaderboard surface. leaderboard/index.html (the "HyperAPI

DocumentAI Benchmark — SEED Leaderboard") is the scientific surface where

named rows are *required to exist* — the naming exception applies. Every

cell carries its provenance tier and [FT] flag. The leaderboard links

out to the paper and dataset card; it never restates a marketing claim.

  • Optional HF Space (human-gated, deferred). A read-only Space rendering

the leaderboard + the §5.1.3 headline chart would let practitioners filter

fine-tuned vs zero-shot rows themselves. It is a *post-approval* item, not

a launch-day dependency — flagged in Open assumptions, not built here.

  • One truthful chart. The launch leads with the §5.1.3 headline figure: a

faithful re-presentation that **deliberately includes the two metric groups

where fine-tuning loses** (precision −0.0165, PaIRS −0.1700). Cropping

those would be the dishonest chart; we ship the honest one.

5. Launch narrative

Hook (two lines): *"We fine-tuned an open VLM for financial-document OCR,

then ran a benchmark honest enough to show where it loses — and a factory

re-run that came back 0/23 green. Here is the real SAVIOR-Bench number, and

why a +0.0276 F1 edge is still the right call."*

Through-line. The surprising-but-true finding is that the fine-tune edge

is *narrow* — and that this is the point, not a disappointment: a modest,

metric-specific, transparently-measured gain on a provenance-tagged benchmark

is worth more than a hyped number nobody can re-derive. The 0/23 W6 result is

told as a feature of the discipline — *the harness refused to fabricate

per-document scores, so it reported red instead of green.* Format-native

variants (HF card prose, an "honest finding" thread, a paper link) all carry

the same spine; competitor names stay inside the scientific artifacts only.

6. How this drives credible virality

  • Reproducibility as the shareable object. HF's audience shares

artifacts that *respect them* — pinned decoding (seed 20260516), a verbatim

LoRA recipe, an explicit ci95=null, and a ✗-marked gap matrix. The W6

0/23 audit is the most screenshot-able asset: it is rare and it signals

integrity.

  • The narrow edge invites scrutiny instead of fearing it. Publishing the

two losing metrics pre-empts the "but actually…" reply that kills hyped

launches; the conversation becomes "this team measures honestly," which

compounds.

  • No vanity metrics. No download/like/star count is claimed or targeted —

the launch has not happened. Success is *quality of engagement* (issues,

citations, re-runs), not inflated counts.

  • Compounding, not a spike. The §10 roadmap (factory re-run, CIs, slices,

degradation tracks) is a public commitment that gives the launch a credible

second and third beat — each closed ✗ is a follow-up moment.
