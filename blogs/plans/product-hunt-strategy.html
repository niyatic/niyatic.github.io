HyperAPI on Product Hunt — Launch Playbook

A DRAFT, human-gated launch plan for putting HyperAPI by Hyperbots —

a document-processing API that turns finance, bank, shipping, and custom

documents into clean structured output for AI/ML pipelines and finance

automation — in front of the Product Hunt audience.

The launch thesis is the same one that governs every Hyperbots artifact: the

honest, non-obvious finding is the asset, not a hype claim. A launch that

spikes the ranking once and erodes trust is a net loss. We optimize for the

credible, compounding kind — within Product Hunt's community rules, with no

vote manipulation and no bought engagement.

Who this is for

Grounded in the HyperAPI ICP: developers and finance-automation teams who feed

documents into AI/ML pipelines. The four document classes HyperAPI converts to

structured output:

  • Finance documents — invoices, AP/AR, statements, expense and tax docs.
  • Bank documents — bank statements and banking/financial records.
  • Shipping documents — bills of lading, customs and logistics paperwork.
  • Custom documents — a customer-defined schema for any document class.

The Product Hunt reader we are writing for is the developer who will paste a

PDF into the playground and check the JSON, and the finance-ops lead who will

forward it to that developer. Both reward specificity and punish hype.

FAQ — pre-empts (drafted answers, posted as makers reply)

  • "How accurate is it?" — Accuracy and SLA are published; we reference them

only per apis.hyperbots.com rather than quoting a figure here. Separately,

our benchmark factory publishes measured, rerunnable model OCR/parse

performance — that is model performance, distinct from product accuracy.

  • "How is this different from OCR-plus-rules / legacy IDP?" — Category

framing only: instead of a template and rule set per vendor, HyperAPI uses

finance-tuned models, so a new layout does not mean new rules. We do not name

or rank specific products in this thread; the governed benchmark artifact is

where named comparisons live.

  • "What models is this?" — Open-source backbones fine-tuned by Hyperbots

for finance documents (Hyperbots IP). We disclose that it is fine-tuned, not

zero-shot, in any public claim.

  • "What does it cost?" — Usage at $0.05/page. Volume and enterprise pricing

are sized on a call; we are not publishing other tiers here.

  • "Is there a human in the loop?" — HyperAPI returns structured output

directly; this is an API, and we describe it as one.

  • "Can it handle my document type?" — Four classes: finance, bank,

shipping, and custom. Custom means a schema you define. If your worst

document breaks it, that is exactly the feedback we want today.

  • "Can I see the benchmark?" — Yes — it is measured, pinned, and

rerunnable; the launch links to how it is run, not just the result.

Credible virality plan (within Product Hunt rules)

The lever is earned attention, never gamed ranking. What we do — and do not

— do:

  • The honest finding is the hook. The shareable line is not "best accuracy

ever." It is "fine-tuned finance models *with the measurement published* —

here is the rerunnable benchmark." Credibility is the thing that travels.

  • Make trying it the call to action. "Try it on your own worst document"

converts curiosity into a real test. Hands-on users write the substantive

comments that genuinely lift a launch.

  • Win the thread with substance. Fast, precise, honest maker replies are

the single biggest legitimate signal. We staff the thread all day.

  • Share, don't solicit. We announce the launch to our real audience on

owned channels and let people decide. We never post a "vote for us" script,

never run an upvote ring, never incentivise or buy votes, never use

sock-puppets — all of which violate Product Hunt's rules and erode trust.

  • Let the benchmark factory do the heavy lifting. Publishing *how* numbers

are measured is itself the differentiated, screenshot-worthy story for a

developer audience that is tired of unverifiable claims.

  • Pre-stage honest assets so anyone who wants to share has something

truthful and well-made to share — no fabricated metrics, no fake urgency.

  • Measure honestly. Any post-launch recap reports only real, verifiable

numbers; no inflated upvote, reach, or signup figure is claimed.
