Classify an inbound finance document into an urgency / SLA tier: one call, a label, and an honest gate

Triage is the quiet tax on finance operations. Inbound finance mail is a firehose: a final-notice with a lien threat, a routine statement, and an FYI copy all land in the same queue. Triage by hand is the bottleneck. This package classifies each document into a single urgency tier so your automation can route by SLA.

The hard part isn't OCR

It's the near-ties. A model can be 0.49 vs 0.46 between two labels and still hand you a confident-looking top class. hyperapi-classify-document-urgency surfaces the per-label scores verbatim, computes the margin between the top and the runner-up, and treats a near-tie as "not yet trustworthy" — exactly like a below-threshold score.

No queue, just a flag

A weak or near-tied first pass is automatically re-classified on a higher-resolution profile; the stronger pass is kept. Documents that stay weak come back trustworthy=false with their scores, for your automation to route by rule. Routing stays in your automation, end to end.

A few lines

``python from hyperapi_classify_document_urgency import classify_file r = classify_file("document.pdf") print(r.classification.label, r.trustworthy) ``

Accuracy is published at apis.hyperbots.com. The model is an open-source document model fine-tuned by Hyperbots (Hyperbots IP); this repo is the typed client around it.
