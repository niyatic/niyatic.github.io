Stop hand-coding non-PO invoices: typed GL category in one call

Non-PO spend is where AP automation quietly stalls. A PO invoice codes itself from the PO; a non-PO bill -- a SaaS renewal, a consultant retainer, a hotel folio, a facilities charge -- still gets a human picking a GL bucket off the page. hyperapi-classify-gl-coding-category turns that into one function call that returns a typed category plus the full ranked distribution.

The hard part isn't reading the page

It's that two categories are often close. A consultancy retainer that mentions a "client portal licence" reads 0.52 professional_services and 0.45 software_subscription -- "confident" by a single number, genuinely ambiguous in fact. This package gates on confidence and the top-vs-runner-up margin, so the ambiguous case is caught and automatically re-classified on a stronger profile.

No queue, just a category

Invoices that stay weak come back trustworthy=false with the full ranking, for your automation to route by rule. An invoice the model cannot place lands in the uncategorized *category* -- a routing target your automation owns, never a person-staffed queue.

Five lines

``python from hyperapi_classify_gl_coding_category import classify_file r = classify_file("invoice.pdf") print(r.decision.category.value, r.trustworthy) ``

Accuracy is published at apis.hyperbots.com. The classifier is an open-source document model fine-tuned by Hyperbots (Hyperbots IP); this repo is the typed client around it.
