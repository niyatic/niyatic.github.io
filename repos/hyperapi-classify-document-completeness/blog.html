The page-1-of-3 statement that quietly short-pays a vendor

A vendor statement prints "Page 1 of 3" in its footer but arrives as a single page. An invoice's total line is sheared off at the page edge. A fax drops its last sheet. Each one extracts "successfully" — into wrong numbers — and the error surfaces as a short payment three steps later. hyperapi-classify-document-completeness puts one deterministic gate in front of extraction.

One question, answered before extraction

Did the whole document arrive? The service returns a verdict — COMPLETE, TRUNCATED, or FRAGMENT — plus the evidence: declared vs. observed page count, the missing page numbers, the section cut off at a page edge. The library normalises the page counts to exact integers and exposes a single complete() boolean your pipeline branches on.

No queue, just a flag

A weak — or self-contradictory — first classification is automatically re-classified on a higher-resolution profile; the stronger pass is kept. Documents that stay weak come back trustworthy=false with the weak signals named, for your automation to route by rule. Routing stays in your automation, end to end.

Five lines

``python from hyperapi_classify_document_completeness import classify_file r = classify_file("document.pdf") print(r.completeness.verdict, r.completeness.complete()) ``

Accuracy is published at apis.hyperbots.com. The model is an open-source document model fine-tuned by Hyperbots (Hyperbots IP); this repo is the typed client around it.
