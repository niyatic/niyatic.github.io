Stop guessing how sensitive a document is: one call, one tier

Privacy and security teams cannot apply the right controls until they know a document's PII-sensitivity tier. A W-9 with a full SSN is a different handling problem than a name-only list, and a card slip pulls it into PCI scope. hyperapi-classify-pii-sensitivity turns that triage into one function call that returns a typed tier with a confidence score on every class.

The hard part isn't OCR

It's that documents rhyme. The library surfaces the service's per-class confidence verbatim and runs a deterministic channel_consistent() check -- every class belongs to exactly one handling regime (none / standard / restricted / special / pci), so a cross-regime flip is caught even when the score looked clean. Classes that share one handling regime are caught by the runner-up margin gate instead.

No queue, just a flag

A borderline first pass -- low top score *or* a thin margin to the runner-up *or* a handling regime conflict -- is automatically re-classified on a higher-resolution profile; the stronger pass is kept. Documents that stay weak come back trustworthy=false for your automation to route by rule. Routing stays in your automation, end to end.

Five lines

``python from hyperapi_classify_pii_sensitivity import classify_file r = classify_file("doc.pdf") print(r.classification.doc_class, r.trustworthy) ``

Accuracy is published at apis.hyperbots.com. The classification model is an open-source document model fine-tuned by Hyperbots (Hyperbots IP); this repo is the typed client around it.
