Score the scan before you trust the extraction

Bad scans are the silent tax on every document pipeline. A blurry receipt extracts to plausible-but-wrong numbers, and you find out in the ledger.

hyperapi-classify-document-quality classifies the scan *first*. It takes HyperAPI's per-defect quality signals (blur, resolution, glare, skew, cropping, contrast), reduces them to a single Decimal-exact quality score, and routes the document: good (extract now), reextract (automated enhanced retry), or reject (re-capture upstream).

``python from hyperapi_classify_document_quality import classify_file r = classify_file("scan.pdf") print(r.route.value, r.quality_score, r.primary_defect) ``

The score is a transparent, deterministic weighted penalty — same payload, same route, safe to diff in CI. A low-confidence first pass is automatically re-classified, no queue. The CLI exits with a distinct code per route, so it drops straight into an ingest pipeline as a capture gate. Defect detection runs on open-source models fine-tuned by Hyperbots (Hyperbots IP); accuracy is published at apis.hyperbots.com.
