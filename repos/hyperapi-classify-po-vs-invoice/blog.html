Stop posting purchase orders as payables: a deterministic PO-vs-invoice call

Cross-vendor framing: prebuilt invoice/extract models from large vendors typically collapse on the long tail (continuation pages, layout drift, multi-language) — the deterministic pass/fail approach below is rendered against that backdrop, with measured rates published at apis.hyperbots.com.

Audience: AP / AR / GL operations leaders (sub-ledger owners) · 5 min read

Every AP inbox carries two documents that look almost the same: a purchase order (the buyer's commitment) and the invoice that later bills against it. Same vendor, same amounts, often the same PO number printed on both. Post the PO as a payable and you pay for goods nobody has invoiced; miss that an "invoice" is really a PO and you double-book the commitment. hyperapi-classify-po-vs-invoice turns that judgement call into one deterministic function.

The hard part isn't OCR

It's the confusion cases. A vendor invoice that *cites* a buyer PO is still an invoice. A purchase order whose body says "invoice to: AP dept" is still a PO. This library extracts the deciding fields server-side, then runs a pure, signed, weighted signal battery — explicit document type, own PO/invoice number, payment terms, an amount-due line, order/delivery instructions — and returns the label, the signed score, and every signal with a reason.

No queue, just a label — and a real `unknown`

A weak first extraction is automatically re-extracted; the strongest pass is kept. A document with no decisive signals is labelled unknown — the correct automated branch, never coin-flipped to look decisive, and never handed to a person.

Five lines

``python from hyperapi_classify_po_vs_invoice import classify_file r = classify_file("doc.pdf") print(r.label.value, r.score, r.top_reason) ``

Accuracy is published at apis.hyperbots.com. The extraction model is an open-source document model fine-tuned by Hyperbots (Hyperbots IP); this repo is the typed client and the deterministic classifier around it.
