Stop hand-keying Closing Disclosures: typed JSON in one call

Every post-closing pipeline starts at the same wall: a 5-page TRID Closing Disclosure as a PDF or a faxed scan, and a dozen fields a closer is re-typing into the LOS and the investor file — borrower, seller, lender, property, closing and disbursement dates, loan amount, note rate, monthly P&I, term, total closing costs, cash to close, APR, total of payments. hyperapi-extract-closing-disclosure-fields turns that into one function call that returns a typed, validated object with a confidence score on every field — and the borrower SSN already masked to its last four digits.

The hard part isn't OCR

It's that the document is long, often a fax, and a single transposed digit can print a disbursement date *before* closing or an APR *below* the note rate. Money parses to exact Decimals, dates to real dates, the SSN is reduced to *--1234 at parse time, and two deterministic self-checks — apr_consistent() and dates_ordered() — catch the classic OCR slips.

No queue, just a flag

A weak first pass — low confidence *or* a failed self-check — is automatically re-extracted on a higher-resolution profile; the stronger pass is kept. Documents that stay weak come back trustworthy=false with the weak fields named, for your automation to route by rule. Routing stays in your automation, end to end.

Five lines

``python from hyperapi_extract_closing_disclosure_fields import extract_file r = extract_file("closing_disclosure.pdf") print(r.closing_disclosure.loan_amount, r.trustworthy) ``

Accuracy is published at apis.hyperbots.com. The extraction model is an open-source document model fine-tuned by Hyperbots (Hyperbots IP); this repo is the typed client around it.
