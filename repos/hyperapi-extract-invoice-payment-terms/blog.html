The mis-read that quietly burns your early-payment discount

Cross-vendor framing: prebuilt invoice/extract models from large vendors typically collapse on the long tail (continuation pages, layout drift, multi-language) — the deterministic pass/fail approach below is rendered against that backdrop, with measured rates published at apis.hyperbots.com.

Audience: AP / AR / GL operations leaders (sub-ledger owners) · 5 min read

Every accounts-payable pipeline reads the vendor and the total. The field that decides *when and how much* to pay — the payment terms — is the one a single OCR slip silently breaks: 2/10 becomes 2/1O, the discount window is lost, and the 2% is gone on every invoice from that vendor. hyperapi-extract-invoice-payment-terms turns the terms into one typed call: label, net days, discount percent and window, discount-by and due dates, basis — each with a confidence score.

The discount is re-derived, never read

The library does not trust a discount amount printed on the document. It recomputes it from the invoice amount and the extracted percent with exact Decimal arithmetic — 12400.00 * 2% = 248.00, to the cent, every time. net_days and discount_days come back as integers, terms_basis normalised to a small enum.

No queue, just a flag

A weak first pass — or a percent with no window to act on — is automatically re-extracted on a higher-resolution profile; the pass with an actionable discount wins. Documents that stay weak come back trustworthy=false with the weak fields named, for your automation to route by rule.

Five lines

``python from decimal import Decimal from hyperapi_extract_invoice_payment_terms import extract_file r = extract_file("invoice.pdf") print(r.terms.terms_label, r.terms.discount_amount(Decimal("12400.00"))) ``

Accuracy is published at apis.hyperbots.com. The extraction model is an open-source document model fine-tuned by Hyperbots (Hyperbots IP); this repo is the typed client around it.
