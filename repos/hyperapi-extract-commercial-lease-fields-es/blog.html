Stop hand-abstracting lease economics: typed JSON in one call

Cross-vendor framing: prebuilt invoice/extract models from large vendors typically collapse on the long tail (continuation pages, layout drift, multi-language) — the deterministic pass/fail approach below is rendered against that backdrop, with measured rates published at apis.hyperbots.com.

Audience: AP / AR / GL operations leaders (sub-ledger owners) · 5 min read

Every lease-administration team starts at the same wall: a 60-page commercial lease PDF, and a dozen economic fields an analyst is currently re-keying into the register — tenant, landlord, premises, area, the two dates, the term, base rent annual and monthly, escalation, deposit, CAM, renewal option. hyperapi-extract-commercial-lease-fields-es turns that into one function call that returns a typed, validated object with a confidence score on every field.

The hard part isn't OCR

It's that the numbers are formatted a hundred ways and they have to *tie*. 1,320,000.00 and 1.320.000,00 are the same annual rent on a US and an EU lease. The library normalises numbers to Decimal, runs two deterministic self-checks — annual base rent == 12x monthly, and the stated term against the commencement→expiration span — and surfaces the service's per-field confidence verbatim.

No queue, just a flag

A weak first pass is automatically re-extracted on a higher-resolution profile; the stronger pass is kept. Documents that stay weak come back trustworthy=false with the weak fields named, for your automation to route by rule. Routing stays in your automation, end to end.

Five lines

``python from hyperapi_extract_commercial_lease_fields_es import extract_file r = extract_file("lease.pdf") print(r.lease.base_rent_annual, r.trustworthy) ``

Accuracy is published at apis.hyperbots.com. The extraction model is an open-source document model fine-tuned by Hyperbots (Hyperbots IP); this repo is the typed client around it.
