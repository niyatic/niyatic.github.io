Pick the right extractor before you extract

Every finance automation pipeline has the same first problem: a PDF shows up and you do not yet know what it is. Run an invoice extractor on a bank statement and you burn a call and get nonsense back. The fix is a cheap, typed classification step that runs *before* extraction.

hyperapi-classify-finance-document-type is one HyperAPI classify call wrapped into a typed decision: a DocumentType enum (invoice, receipt, bank statement, PO, remittance, W-9, BOL, credit memo, requisition), the model's calibrated confidence surfaced verbatim, and a RoutingHint that names the downstream extractor. High-confidence known types are auto_routable. Everything else — low confidence, thin margin, or out-of-vocabulary — is flagged for confidence-gated automatic re-submission, not pushed downstream as a guess.

The interesting work is the boring-looking edge: invoice vs. credit memo. They look almost identical, so the model can put a confident-looking label on a near-split distribution. The package exposes the top-vs-runner-up margin and refuses to auto-route a sub-threshold result, which is exactly the case you do not want silently mis-routed.

The classifier is an open-source model fine-tuned by Hyperbots on finance-document corpora (Hyperbots IP). Accuracy and calibration are published per apis.hyperbots.com; this package reproduces those numbers without modification.
