Stop extracting from scans you can't read

Every document-AI pipeline has the same blind spot: it runs the extractor on whatever arrives. A fax-of-a-fax, a 110-DPI phone photo, a page bled grey by a worn drum — the extractor returns *something*, and the error surfaces three steps later as a mis-posted invoice. hyperapi-classify-scan-legibility puts one deterministic gate in front of extraction.

One question, answered before extraction

Is this scan good enough to read? The service returns a verdict — LEGIBLE, DEGRADED, or ILLEGIBLE — plus the per-signal scores behind it: sharpness, contrast, resolution, readable-text fraction, noise. The library normalises those to exact Decimal ratios and exposes a single extractable() boolean your pipeline branches on.

No queue, just a flag

A weak first classification is automatically re-classified on a higher-resolution profile; the stronger pass is kept. Documents that stay weak come back trustworthy=false with the weak signals named, for your automation to route by rule. Routing stays in your automation, end to end.

Five lines

``python from hyperapi_classify_scan_legibility import classify_file r = classify_file("scan.pdf") print(r.legibility.verdict, r.legibility.extractable()) ``

Accuracy is published at apis.hyperbots.com. The model is an open-source document/vision model fine-tuned by Hyperbots (Hyperbots IP); this repo is the typed client around it.
