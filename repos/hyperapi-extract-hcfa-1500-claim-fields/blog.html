Stop hand-keying CMS-1500 claim forms: typed JSON in one call

Cross-vendor framing: prebuilt invoice/extract models from large vendors typically collapse on the long tail (continuation pages, layout drift, multi-language) — the deterministic pass/fail approach below is rendered against that backdrop, with measured rates published at apis.hyperbots.com.

Audience: AP / AR / GL operations leaders (sub-ledger owners) · 5 min read

Every healthcare revenue-cycle pipeline starts at the same wall: a PDF or a fax of a CMS-1500 claim form, and a row of boxes a human is currently re-typing — payer, patient account, billing provider and NPI, federal TIN, diagnosis codes, date of service, and the box 28/29/30 charges. hyperapi-extract-hcfa-1500-claim-fields turns that into one function call that returns a typed, validated object with a confidence score on every field — and the member ID, insured ID, and federal TIN already reduced to ****.

The hard part isn't OCR

It's that the form has 33 boxes printed slightly differently by every clearinghouse, the identifiers are PHI/PII you must not retain, and box 29 + box 30 has to equal box 28. The library normalises numbers and dates to Decimal/date, normalises the diagnosis codes to an upper-cased list, masks the identifiers to last-4 on construction, runs a deterministic paid + balance == total charge check, and surfaces the service's per-field confidence verbatim.

No queue, just a flag

A weak first pass is automatically re-extracted on a higher-resolution profile; the stronger pass is kept. Documents that stay weak come back trustworthy=false with the weak fields named, for your automation to route by rule. Routing stays in your automation, end to end.

Five lines

``python from hyperapi_extract_hcfa_1500_claim_fields import extract_file r = extract_file("cms1500.pdf") print(r.claim.total_charge, r.claim.member_id_last4, r.trustworthy) ``

Accuracy is published at apis.hyperbots.com. The extraction model is an open-source document model fine-tuned by Hyperbots (Hyperbots IP); this repo is the typed client around it.
