Stop hand-sorting contracts: one typed label in one call

Every contract pipeline starts at the same fork: a PDF or a phone photo, and a human deciding which kind of contract it is before anything else can happen. hyperapi-classify-contract-subtype-hi turns that into one function call that returns a typed subtype label with a calibrated score on every class.

The hard part isn't OCR

It's that two subtypes can look almost identical on a bad scan. The library surfaces the service's per-class scores verbatim, computes the margin between the top two, and automatically re-classifies on a higher-resolution profile when that margin is thin — keeping whichever pass separates the classes better.

No queue, just a flag

A weak or close first pass is re-classified automatically. Documents that stay ambiguous come back trustworthy=false for your automation to route by rule. Routing stays in your automation, end to end.

Five lines

``python from hyperapi_classify_contract_subtype_hi import classify_file r = classify_file("contract.pdf") print(r.classification.subtype, r.trustworthy) ``

Accuracy is published at apis.hyperbots.com. The classification model is an open-source document model fine-tuned by Hyperbots (Hyperbots IP); this repo is the typed client around it.
