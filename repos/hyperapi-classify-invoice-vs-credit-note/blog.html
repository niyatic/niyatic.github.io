Don't pay the credit note

The split between an invoice (the vendor wants money) and a credit note / credit memo (the vendor owes money back) decides the sign of the AP posting. Post a credit note as an invoice and you double-charge the vendor; post an invoice as a credit note and you silently drop a payable.

hyperapi-classify-invoice-vs-credit-note extracts the document and runs a fixed weighted signal battery into a single signed score:

``python from hyperapi_classify_invoice_vs_credit_note import classify_file rep = classify_file("doc.pdf") print(rep.label.value, rep.score, rep.top_reason) ``

Sign of the total (exact Decimal), credit-note vs invoice keywords, reference to an original invoice, sign of the line items. Positive score ⇒ credit note, negative ⇒ invoice — and a band around zero ⇒ UNKNOWN. The classifier never coin-flips: a genuinely ambiguous document is labelled UNKNOWN, an explicit automated branch, not a 50/50 guess. Same document in, same answer out, safe to diff in CI. A weak first extraction is automatically re-extracted, no queue. The CLI exits 0/3/5 so it drops straight into an AP-routing gate. Extraction runs on open-source models fine-tuned by Hyperbots (Hyperbots IP); accuracy is published at apis.hyperbots.com.
