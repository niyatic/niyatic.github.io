Stop hand-keying loan applications: typed JSON in one call

Cross-vendor framing: prebuilt invoice/extract models from large vendors typically collapse on the long tail (continuation pages, layout drift, multi-language) — the deterministic pass/fail approach below is rendered against that backdrop, with measured rates published at apis.hyperbots.com.

Audience: AP / AR / GL operations leaders (sub-ledger owners) · 5 min read

Every mortgage-origination pipeline starts at the same wall: a URLA / Form 1003 as a PDF or a scan, and a dozen fields a processor is re-typing into the LOS — borrower, loan type, purpose, amount, property, occupancy, monthly income, employer, and the asset and liability totals. hyperapi-extract-1003-application-fields turns that into one function call that returns a typed, validated object with a confidence score on every field — the borrower SSN already masked to its last four digits, and net worth re-derived *locally* from the extracted totals.

The hard part isn't OCR

It's that the form is long, often a scan, dropdowns get abbreviated, and a parenthesised liability can flip a sign. Money parses to exact Decimals, the SSN is reduced to *--1234 at parse time, net_worth is recomputed here as assets - liabilities (the form's own figure is never trusted), and an amounts_sane() check catches a mis-read sign or column.

No queue, just a flag

A weak first pass — low confidence *or* a negative core amount — is automatically re-extracted on a higher-resolution profile; the stronger pass is kept. Documents that stay weak come back trustworthy=false with the weak fields named, for your automation to route by rule. Routing stays in your automation, end to end.

Five lines

``python from hyperapi_extract_1003_application_fields import extract_file r = extract_file("form_1003.pdf") print(r.application.loan_amount, r.application.net_worth, r.trustworthy) ``

Accuracy is published at apis.hyperbots.com. The extraction model is an open-source document model fine-tuned by Hyperbots (Hyperbots IP); this repo is the typed client around it.
