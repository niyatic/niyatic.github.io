Stop hand-keying intercompany loan notes: typed JSON in one call

Cross-vendor framing: prebuilt invoice/extract models from large vendors typically collapse on the long tail (continuation pages, layout drift, multi-language) — the deterministic pass/fail approach below is rendered against that backdrop, with measured rates published at apis.hyperbots.com.

Audience: AP / AR / GL operations leaders (sub-ledger owners) · 5 min read

Intercompany loan notes are low-volume but high-consequence: they drive transfer-pricing interest, group eliminations, and the related-party disclosures auditors test first. Yet the terms are still re-typed off signed PDFs and scans — note reference, lender, borrower, principal, dates, rate, repayment. hyperapi-extract-intercompany-loan-note turns that into one function call that returns a typed, validated object with a confidence score on every field.

The hard part isn't OCR

It's that a mis-read principal or rate looks just as confident as a correct one. The library normalises numbers, dates, and percents to Decimal/date, then re-derives the simple interest locally (principal × rate × days/365, exact Decimal) and cross-checks it against the figure the note states. A note where the maths doesn't close is flagged even when every field scored high.

No queue, just a flag

A weak first pass — or one whose terms don't self-check — is automatically re-extracted on a higher-resolution profile; the stronger pass is kept. Documents that stay weak come back trustworthy=false with the weak fields named, for your automation to route by rule. Routing stays in your automation, end to end.

Five lines

``python from hyperapi_extract_intercompany_loan_note import extract_file r = extract_file("loan_note.pdf") print(r.loan_note.principal, r.loan_note.terms_consistent()) ``

Accuracy is published at apis.hyperbots.com. The extraction model is an open-source document model fine-tuned by Hyperbots (Hyperbots IP); the interest re-derivation is this repo.
