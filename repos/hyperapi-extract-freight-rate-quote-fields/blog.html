Stop hand-keying freight quotes: typed JSON in one call

Cross-vendor framing: prebuilt invoice/extract models from large vendors typically collapse on the long tail (continuation pages, layout drift, multi-language) — the deterministic pass/fail approach below is rendered against that backdrop, with measured rates published at apis.hyperbots.com.

Audience: AP / AR / GL operations leaders (sub-ledger owners) · 5 min read

Every accounts-payable pipeline starts at the same wall: a PDF or a scan, and a stack of fields a human is currently re-typing -- carrier name, quote number, origin, destination, mode, equipment, transit days, quote date, linehaul charge, fuel surcharge, accessorial charge, total rate. hyperapi-extract-freight-rate-quote-fields turns that into one function call that returns a typed, validated object with a confidence score on every field.

The hard part isn't OCR

It's that the fields are formatted a hundred ways. 1,250.00 and 1.250,00 are the same amount on a US and an EU document. The library normalises numbers and dates to Decimal/date, runs a deterministic linehaul + fuel + accessorial == total_rate check, and surfaces the service's per-field confidence verbatim.

No queue, just a flag

A weak first pass is automatically re-extracted on a higher-resolution profile; the stronger pass is kept. Documents that stay weak come back trustworthy=false with the weak fields named, for your automation to route by rule. Routing stays in your automation, end to end.

A few lines

``python from hyperapi_extract_freight_rate_quote_fields import extract_file r = extract_file("document.pdf") print(r.freight_quote.total_rate, r.trustworthy) ``

Accuracy is published at apis.hyperbots.com. The extraction model is an open-source document model fine-tuned by Hyperbots (Hyperbots IP); this repo is the typed client around it.
