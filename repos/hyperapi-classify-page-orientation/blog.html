The sideways page that breaks your extractor

Scanners and phone cameras rotate pages without asking: a landscape invoice fed sideways, a duplex page flipped 180°, a portrait photo of a landscape print. Every one reads as garbage to an extractor that assumes upright text — and the failure is silent. hyperapi-classify-page-orientation puts one deterministic deskew gate in front of extraction.

Per page, an exact correction angle

The service returns, for each page, its rotation — 0, 90, 180, or 270° — and a confidence. The library snaps any out-of-range reported angle to the nearest quadrant and derives the exact clockwise correction angle as an integer (never a float) — feed it straight into your image-rotate step.

No queue, just a flag

A low-confidence first pass is automatically re-classified on a higher-resolution profile; the stronger pass is kept. Documents that stay weak come back trustworthy=false with the misoriented pages listed, for your automation to route. Routing stays in your automation, end to end.

Five lines

``python from hyperapi_classify_page_orientation import classify_file r = classify_file("scan.pdf") print(r.report.all_upright(), r.report.misoriented_pages()) ``

Accuracy is published at apis.hyperbots.com. The model is an open-source document/vision model fine-tuned by Hyperbots (Hyperbots IP); this repo is the typed client around it.
