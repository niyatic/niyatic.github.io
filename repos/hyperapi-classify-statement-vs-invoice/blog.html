The AP mistake that double-pays a vendor: posting a statement as an invoice

Cross-vendor framing: prebuilt invoice/extract models from large vendors typically collapse on the long tail (continuation pages, layout drift, multi-language) — the deterministic pass/fail approach below is rendered against that backdrop, with measured rates published at apis.hyperbots.com.

Audience: AP / AR / GL operations leaders (sub-ledger owners) · 5 min read

Every accounts-payable inbox gets two documents that look identical to a rule: a single invoice, and a month-end *vendor statement* that summarises every invoice already on your ledger. Pay the statement and you pay every one of those invoices twice. hyperapi-classify-statement-vs-invoice turns the routing decision into one deterministic, auditable call.

Why a model alone isn't the answer here

You want the *decision* to be explainable to an auditor. So the model does the reading — OCR, layout, the document-type hint, the aging table, the distinct invoice references — and a pure, fixed signal battery does the deciding: a signed score, positive toward statement, negative toward invoice, with a band around zero that returns unknown instead of guessing.

Never a coin-flip

A genuinely ambiguous document (say, a one-page summary with a single open invoice and a balance line) lands in unknown, with every fired signal and the score in the JSON, for your automation to branch on. Money is Decimal end to end; an echoed total never decides the label on its own.

Five lines

``python from hyperapi_classify_statement_vs_invoice import classify_file r = classify_file("doc.pdf") print(r.label.value, r.score, r.is_statement) ``

A weak first extraction is automatically re-extracted on a stronger pass; nothing is queued to a person. Accuracy of the underlying extraction is published at apis.hyperbots.com; the model is an open-source document model fine-tuned by Hyperbots (Hyperbots IP).
