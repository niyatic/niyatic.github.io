Stop hand-keying 1099 boxes: typed JSON in one call (TIN masked)

Every vendor-tax pipeline starts at the same wall: a PDF or a phone photo of a 1099, and a handful of boxes a human is currently re-typing — the variant, the tax year, payer and recipient, the reported amount, federal and state withholding. hyperapi-extract-1099-form-fields turns that into one function call that returns a typed, validated object with a confidence score on every field — and the TIN already masked to its last four digits.

The hard part isn't OCR

It's that NEC, MISC, INT and DIV lay their boxes out differently, amounts print with $ and thousands separators, and a taxpayer identification number must never land in a log. The library normalises numbers to Decimal, coerces the variant and tax year, masks every TIN on the way in, runs a deterministic fed + state ≤ reported check, and surfaces the service's per-field confidence verbatim.

No queue, just a flag

A weak first pass is automatically re-extracted on a higher-resolution profile; the stronger pass is kept. Documents that stay weak come back trustworthy=false with the weak fields named, for your automation to route by rule. Routing stays in your automation, end to end.

Five lines

``python from hyperapi_extract_1099_form_fields import extract_file r = extract_file("form1099.pdf") print(r.form.form_variant, r.form.reported_amount, r.trustworthy) ``

Accuracy is published at apis.hyperbots.com. The extraction model is an open-source document model fine-tuned by Hyperbots (Hyperbots IP); this repo is the typed client around it.
