Stop misfiling tax forms: which IRS form is it, in one call

Vendor onboarding runs on tax paperwork, and the first question is always the same: *which form is this?* A new payee uploads "the tax form" and it might be a W-9, a W-8BEN, a W-8BEN-E, a W-4, or last year's 1099 — and each one routes to a different workflow. File a W-8BEN as a W-9 and you apply the wrong withholding. hyperapi-classify-tax-form-type turns that first question into one function call that returns a typed label with a confidence score on every class.

The hard part isn't OCR

It's that the forms rhyme. A faxed 1099-NEC and 1099-MISC differ by one box; a W-8BEN and W-8BEN-E differ by individual-vs-entity. The library surfaces the service's per-class confidence verbatim and runs a deterministic family_consistent() check — every form belongs to exactly one family (payee-certificate, withholding, information-return), so a cross-family flip is caught even when the score looked clean.

No queue, just a flag

A borderline first pass — low top score *or* a thin margin to the runner-up *or* a family conflict — is automatically re-classified on a higher-resolution profile; the stronger pass is kept. Documents that stay weak come back trustworthy=false for your automation to route by rule. Routing stays in your automation, end to end.

Five lines

``python from hyperapi_classify_tax_form_type import classify_file r = classify_file("form.pdf") print(r.classification.form, r.trustworthy) ``

Accuracy is published at apis.hyperbots.com. The classification model is an open-source document model fine-tuned by Hyperbots (Hyperbots IP); this repo is the typed client around it.
