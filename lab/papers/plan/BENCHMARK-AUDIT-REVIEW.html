1. Integrity risks (with line refs)

CRITICAL — must fix before any human posts

I-1. 23 vs 24 parse-intent model count — internal self-contradiction. The parse-intent panel is 23 models, verified three independent ways: (a) results/DC-000__parse-intent-inhouse-v1__*.json → 23 files; (b) benchmark.xlsx sheet intent → 24 rows = 1 header + 23 models; (c) paper/SAVIOR-Bench.md §5.1.1 table itself ends at Rank 23 (L261–262); (d) paper/HF-CARD.md L72 correctly says "23-model". But the paper says "24" in five places: abstract L40 ("24 systems"), contributions L90 ("~24 parse-intent systems"), related-work matrix L194 ("~24 parse"), experimental setup L215 ("~24 parse-intent systems"), and Sources L693 ("(24 cells)"). A skeptical reader counts the table to 23, sees "24" in the abstract, and concludes the authors did not check their own paper — cheap, fatal credibility damage on a benchmark whose entire pitch is rigor. Fix: change all five to 23 (the table, the JSON, the xlsx, and the HF card are the ground truth; the prose is wrong).

I-2. Missing primary source: Benchmark-VLM_OCR.pdf. Cited throughout as the source for IAA 0.761 (L132, L580), document classes (L130–131), corpus mix (§2.1), latency p50/p99/fail (§5.4 L330–338), cost (§5.5 L342–349), the LoRA recipe (Appendix A L549), failure modes (§5.6 L353), and n=509. It is not present at the cited Desktop path, nor anywhere under the repo / ~/Desktop / ~/Documents (searched). Only benchmark.xlsx exists — and the xlsx contains only the model score tables (F1/WER, Recall/PaIRS/Precision): it has no IAA, no latency, no cost, no LoRA recipe, no failure modes, no corpus mix, no n. So every non-leaderboard number in the paper currently traces to an artifact that cannot be produced on demand. This is not (yet) fabrication — the framing is honestly "user-attested 2026-05-18" — but "attested to a source we cannot show you" is exactly the provenance shape that detonates under scrutiny. Fix (research agent): either (a) locate/commit a redacted Benchmark-VLM_OCR.pdf (or its data extract) into the repo so the attested numbers have a recoverable artifact, or (b) downgrade every PDF-only number to an explicitly flagged "attested, source not in repository" tier and stop citing a file path that does not resolve. Option (a) is strongly preferred before any public post.

MEDIUM

I-3. DocIntentOCR-3B zero-shot F1 0.8102 with WER 0.5622 (§5.1.1 L240). This is the #2 system and the paper's designated "best zero-shot" comparator — it carries the entire honesty narrative ("fine-tune edge is only +0.0226 over best zero-shot"). It is an obscure 3B model beating GPT-4o on F1 zero-shot. The number matches the xlsx exactly (no fabrication), but the paper gives zero provenance for what DocIntentOCR-3B even is (no vendor, no version, no checkpoint, no config — Appendix D itself admits configs are missing for some rows, §10 R6 "partial"). The entire modesty framing leans on a black-box row. HN will ask "what is DocIntentOCR-3B and why does a 3B model beat GPT-4o zero-shot on your task." Fix: add a one-line provenance note for DocIntentOCR-3B (source/checkpoint/date), or explicitly flag it as an under-documented panel row and re-anchor the modesty claim on GPT-4o (0.8052, well-known) as the best zero-shot reference instead. GPT-4o is a far more defensible "best zero-shot" anchor for public copy.

I-4. PaIRS leaders are NPC, headline contrast mixes PC/NPC. Abstract L46 and Case Study 2 (L438–448) lean on "PaddleOCR-VL-1.5 0.8766 PaIRS vs Textract 0.5117" — but both are NPC, and the paper's own §2.2/§8 rule says NPC PaIRS is a format-compat proxy, not comparable. The paper does disclose this inline (L446–448, honest), but the abstract (L46) states the PaIRS leaders without the NPC caveat. An abstract-only reader gets a comparability claim the body retracts. Fix: add the PC/NPC caveat to the abstract sentence, or drop PaIRS rankings from the abstract entirely.

I-5. Leaderboard names competitors on a web surface, not just "the benchmark artifact." leaderboard/index.html embeds and renders Claude Sonnet 4.6, GPT-4o, Gemini-2.5-Pro, DeepSeek-OCR-2, etc. as ranked rows. The paper's naming exception (L102–106) is scoped to "this benchmark artifact." A standalone HTML leaderboard is arguably a second artifact and a more marketing-adjacent surface than a paper. Fix (governance): confirm in one explicit line (in the leaderboard warn box or spec) that the scientific-naming exception covers the leaderboard page too; otherwise this is the "named competitors outside the artifact" guardrail breach the brief flags.

2. Virality merit (what earns shares vs gets dunked)

What works (keep)

  • The inverted, self-deprecating framing ("fine-tuning buys only

+0.0061 recall over Textract; zero-shot GPT-4o wins precision; we do NOT lead layout") is the rare honest-flex that technical audiences upvote. This is the asset. A vendor publishing a benchmark where its own model loses on 2 of 4 metrics is the surprising-but-true thing.

  • §3 related-work matrix with ✗ self-marks is screenshot-worthy *in the

good way* — it reads as confidence, not weakness.

  • The reproduction-status ASCII box (Appendix D) is the kind of artifact

that earns "ok these people are serious" replies.

Virality weaknesses (fix for the launch post the other agent must write)

  • V-1. No single honest hook is foregrounded. The paper buries its best

shareable line ("we built a finance OCR benchmark and our own fine-tune only beats off-the-shelf Textract by 0.6% on recall — and loses precision and layout to zero-shot models") under 727 lines of hedging. The launch post does not exist yet; whoever writes it must lead with this.

  • V-2. The leaderboard is not screenshot-optimized. A skeptical

engineer screenshots ONE table. The current index.html is a filterable data grid with family:"unknown" — not a clean ranked hero chart. There is no single honest "one chart." The funnel needs one chart: a grouped bar of best-zero-shot vs the [FT] row across Recall / Precision / PaIRS / parse-F1, which visually shows fine-tune winning only 2 of 4 — that chart IS the hook and is honest.

  • **V-3. Naming GPT-4o / Claude / Gemini IS the share driver AND the

biggest dunk surface.** People share competitive numbers; competitors' communities screenshot-and-dunk if anything is unfair. Current mitigation (zero-shot label, [FT] tags, dated/versioned) is strong — but I-3 (black-box DocIntentOCR-3B as #2) and I-4 (abstract PC/NPC mixing) are the two threads a hostile reader pulls. Fix those and the competitive framing becomes net-positive virality.

  • V-4. CTA is weak. Only "benchmark access on request:

niyati.chhaya@hyprbots.com" (L136, HF-CARD L120). For 1000 signups in 24h you need a clean self-serve signup, not a personal email mailto. This is a funnel-design gap for the (not-yet-written) launch artifacts, not a paper bug.

3. Recommended single strongest honest hook

> "We built a 509-doc financial-document OCR benchmark, put our own > fine-tuned model on it next to GPT-4o, Gemini, Claude, Textract and 20+ > others — and our model only beats off-the-shelf Textract by 0.6% on > recall, loses precision to zero-shot GPT-4o, and doesn't lead layout at > all. Here's the leaderboard and exactly how it's scored."

This is the genuinely surprising-but-true finding (a vendor benchmark where the vendor's model is not dominant), it is fully supported by the attested data, it is dunk-resistant because it under-claims, and it funnels naturally to "see the full leaderboard / get access." Pair it with one honest chart (V-2: grouped bars, best-zero-shot vs [FT] across the four metrics, showing 2-of-4). Do not lead with "hyperapi-parse-intent is

#1" — that is the framing that invites the apples-to-apples dunk.
