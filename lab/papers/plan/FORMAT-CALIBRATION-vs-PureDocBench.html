SAVIOR-Bench paper — format calibration vs PureDocBench (arXiv 2605.07492)

_User asked: refer the parse benchmark paper against PureDocBench from a format perspective — "can the paper be so deep?" Honest answer below. Governed by benchmark-format-steward + the econometrics-AI statistical conscience. The honesty bar applies to this assessment itself._

0. Honest one-line verdict

No — not today, and saying it could would be dishonest. PureDocBench's depth is earned by work we have not done (a regenerable source-traceable dataset, reproduced numbers, 40 models, per-track/-category/-degradation slices, a prior-benchmark audit). We can match its format discipline; we cannot match its evidence depth without doing that underlying work, and the paper must say so plainly rather than imitate the shape without the substance.

2. So: what P2.5 can legitimately deepen (format parity, no fabrication)

  1. Restructure to a research spine (Intro → Benchmark Construction → Experiments → Analysis → Case Studies → Conclusion/Limitations/Future → Appendices A–n).
  2. Add the related-work comparison matrix (SAVIOR-Bench vs public doc-AI benchmarks × infra dims) — honestly marking ✗ where we don't yet satisfy a dim (verifiability/regenerability/reproduced).
  3. Fixture/annotation audit appendix — audit our own ground truth (IAA 0.761, 187 pairs, mismatch analysis); never claim a third-party audit.
  4. Error/failure taxonomy — formalize Pillar 6 into a classed taxonomy with counts where the attested data supports it; "no per-class counts available" where it doesn't.
  5. Honest case studies — 2–4 worked examples from existing fixtures with failure annotations (no new data).
  6. Methodology + reproducibility appendix — per-model configs we have, frozen prompt, decoding pins, and an explicit "reproduction status: attested-not-factory-reproduced" box (not hidden).
  7. A "Path to PureDocBench-depth" section — the explicit roadmap of what evidence work (factory re-reproduction, per-category slices, degradation tracks, regenerable corpus, broader model coverage) would close the gap. This is the honest way to "be that deep": name the gap and the path, don't cosplay the shape.

3. What P2.5 must NOT do (hard)

No fabricated per-category/degradation slices; no claimed reproduction of attested cells; no invented figures/data points; no claimed third-party benchmark audit; no model-count inflation; competitor naming stays scoped to this artifact, neutral + dated; fine-tune-vs-zero-shot stays first-block. Depth that isn't earned is not added.
