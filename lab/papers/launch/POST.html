Variant A — Hacker News (Show HN)

Title: Show HN: SAVIOR-Bench – 509 financial docs, 45 OCR/VLM systems, and a fine-tune edge smaller than we expected

Body:

We build document-AI for finance, so we needed to know how good OCR/VLM stacks actually are on real financial documents — invoices, packing slips, forms, multilingual scans, low-quality PDFs, dense tables. We couldn't find a benchmark that scored both whole-token transcription fidelity and reading-order/layout fidelity on a finance corpus, so we built one.

SAVIOR-Bench v1: 509 expert-annotated documents across five corpora (UCSF Industry Documents, in-house synthetic, misc. public financial, Inv3D, FUNSD), inter-annotator agreement 0.761 over 187 double-annotated pairs. We score word-level multiset recall/precision, a pairwise spatial-relation layout score (PaIRS), and a parse-intent F1/WER, with one frozen prompt and pinned decoding per task family, across ~21 OCR and 23 parse-intent systems (cloud document-AI APIs, frontier multimodal LLMs, open-source OCR/VLM).

The honest headline: we LoRA-fine-tuned open Qwen2.5-VL backbones on our training split. On OCR recall the fine-tuned model leads the best zero-shot system by +0.0061 (0.9294 vs 0.9233). On parse-intent it leads the best well-documented zero-shot system by +0.0276 F1 (0.8328 vs 0.8052) — +0.0226 vs a marginally-higher but under-documented panel row — with a much lower WER (named systems are in the linked paper). On OCR precision and layout, zero-shot systems actually win — fine-tuning buys a narrow, metric-specific recall/F1 edge, not a universal one. We say this first in the abstract; the three fine-tuned rows are tagged [FT] everywhere.

Two more findings we didn't expect: recall and layout decouple hard (a system can be near-bottom on recall and first on reading-order, and vice versa — which is exactly why we score both), and verbose hallucination inflates transcription WER while field-F1 survives, so reporting only one metric hides a real failure mode.

What it is not, said plainly: numbers are in-house-run and user-attested (2026-05-18), not yet re-run under our deterministic factory harness (the harness exists; re-reproduction is the next hardening step, not a hidden caveat). No confidence intervals (ci95=null, not faked). No per-class slices (the attested source is corpus-aggregate; we mark that gap rather than fabricate splits). Full related-work matrix marks every dimension we don't yet satisfy with an ✗.

The fine-tune edge over the best zero-shot model:
