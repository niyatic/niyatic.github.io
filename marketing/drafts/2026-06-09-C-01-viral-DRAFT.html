We benchmarked our fine-tune against the leading LLM. The honest number is +0.0276 F1.

Most vendor LinkedIn posts you'll see this quarter quote a "10× better than the leading LLM" line. We ran the benchmark. The honest number is +0.0276 F1.

That's 27 thousandths. It is a small number. We published the sentence anyway — because the credibility of every other claim we make depends on it.

SAVIOR-Bench v1, parse-intent task (finance-document intent + structured-field reading; attested 2026-05-18, ci95=null):

  • Our fine-tune [FT] (open-source backbone fine-tuned by Hyperbots, Hyperbots IP) — F1 0.8328 · WER 0.2589
  • GPT-4o (zero-shot, foundation-model baseline) — F1 0.8052 · WER 0.4232
  • Delta: +0.0276 F1, −0.1643 WER

The F1 lead is small. The WER drop is the part most "we win" posts miss: the same fine-tune that nudges F1 up by 0.0276 cuts word-error-rate by roughly 39% relative. F1 measures whether the right fields ended up in the right slots; WER measures whether the digits inside them are correct. For finance documents, getting the right total in the right slot but transcribing the digits wrong is a downstream-pipeline poison. The big number is the WER cut, not the F1 nudge.

Where we lose, also published: on the OCR task in the same panel, the best zero-shot frontier system beats our fine-tune on precision (−0.0165) and PaIRS (−0.1700). And on a different task — full-pipeline invoice extract on dataset_95 — our IDP chain lands at 0.3573, below both Gemini Flash and Pro. We're not hiding those.

The bet isn't the model. The bet is: small open backbones fine-tuned on a deep, expert-annotated finance corpus, served per primitive (parse / classify / split / match / validate / augment / reason), at predictable economics, with a published-failure-honesty audit (W6 audit re-derived 0/23 cells — we shipped that too).

A small published number with the losses shown beside it is doing something different than "10× better." It's signaling a credibility discipline, not a magnitude claim.

If you evaluate document AI: the honest test is your own corpus. Pick the primitive your workflow lives or dies on, and run the head-to-head on your documents. Whoever publishes their losing rows alongside their winning rows is the one telling you something useful.
