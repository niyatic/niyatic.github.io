We published a benchmark — and the audit that says we haven't reproduced it yet. On purpose.

SAVIOR-Bench v1 is a 509-document, expert-annotated financial-document benchmark: 21 OCR systems, 23 parse-intent systems, one frozen prompt per task family, decoding pinned to a seed. Inter-annotator agreement is 0.761 across 187 double-annotated pairs. Standard rigor, so far.

Here's the part most teams bury. Every cell in the report carries one label: ATTESTED — NOT FACTORY-REPRODUCED. The numbers were run in-house and signed on 2026-05-18. The deterministic factory harness, the pinned environment, and the repro-check script all exist — but they have not yet been run against these specific cells. We say that on every row, not in a footnote.

Then we tried. The W6 workstream re-ran all 23 parse-intent rows through the pinned harness. The outcome: 0 of 23 re-derived. Twenty-three reds — because the open SEED harness has no adapters for hosted-API vendors, the in-house corpus isn't redistributable, and per-document scores don't exist yet. Zero rows retracted, zero confidence intervals invented, zero status flips. We published that table next to the results.

The discipline is enforced in code. The registered-but-unrun tabular stream raises NotImplementedError by design — no number can leak into a slide. Confidence intervals are null, not a fabricated interval.

Reproducibility is a status, not an adjective. If you're evaluating document-AI vendors, ask which of their numbers are attested and which are reproduced — and whether they'll show you the audit. The full SAVIOR-Bench methodology and the W6 audit are available for review.

The No-Faked-Cell Discipline

Deep technical blog · ~1,900 words · angle: methodology & reproducibility rigor.

1. The problem with the word "reproducible"

"Reproducible" is the most over-claimed word in applied ML. It appears in abstracts that ship no harness, in leaderboards whose numbers were transcribed from a spreadsheet nobody can re-run, and in vendor decks where a single favorable cell has been silently rounded up. The word has become a vibe rather than a verifiable state.

SAVIOR-Bench v1 — a 509-document, expert-annotated financial-document benchmark spanning 21 OCR systems and 23 parse-intent systems — was built around a refusal to use the word that way. The governing rule is blunt: a cell that cannot be computed honestly is not estimated, it raises an error. We call this the no-faked-cell discipline, and this post is about how it is enforced in code, what it cost us, and why a benchmark that openly admits it has not been factory-reproduced is more trustworthy than one that claims it has.

2. The setup: ordinary rigor first

Before the interesting part, the table stakes. SAVIOR-Bench v1 holds 509 financial documents drawn from five corpora — UCSF Industry Documents (46.95%), in-house synthetic (17.88%), miscellaneous public financial (17.49%), Inv3D (9.04%), and FUNSD (8.64%) — across six document classes: invoices, packing slips, forms, multilingual scans, low-quality PDFs, and complex tables.

Scoring is defined in code, not prose. OCR is word-level multiset recall and precision over whole tokens — character-level CER was deliberately rejected, because the downstream consumer is an LLM extraction step that ingests whole tokens, so Totl versus Total earns a full penalty, not a partial one. Layout fidelity uses PaIRS, a quadratic spatial penalty over pairwise token positions, and it is reported only for prompt-controlled (PC) models; for non-prompt-controlled (NPC) systems PaIRS is explicitly downgraded to a format-compatibility proxy, not a comparable ranking. Parse-intent uses F1 over extracted intent fields and WER over the transcription.

Decoding is pinned: temperature 0.1, top-p 0.9, max_tokens 2048, empty stop list, seed 20260516, recorded in harness/tasks.json under task DC-000. One frozen prompt template per task family is used across every model; model-specific chat formatting is confined to adapters so it cannot become an accidental tuning surface. Inter-annotator agreement is 0.761 over 187 double-annotated pairs. This is the floor — and most benchmarks stop here, then write "reproducible" in the abstract.

3. The honest label: attested is not reproduced

SAVIOR-Bench v1 does not stop there, because stopping there would require a claim it cannot back. Every cell in the report carries a verbatim reproduction-status block:

REPRODUCTION STATUS: ATTESTED — NOT FACTORY-REPRODUCED
  provenance            : in-house-run, user-attested 2026-05-18
  reproduced_by_factory : false
  ci95                  : null   (shown "reported (no CI)")
  rerun_command         : "in-house run; user-attested 2026-05-18
                           - not reproduced by the factory harness"

Two words are kept rigorously separate. Attested means a named party ran the numbers and signed them on a date. Reproduced means the deterministic factory harness re-derived them from inputs under a pinned environment and a green repro-check. SAVIOR-Bench v1 is the first. It is not yet the second. The harness, the pinned environment (harness/PINS.txt), and the verifier (harness/repro_check.py) are all defined and in place — they simply have not been run against these specific cells. The report says this on every results row, not once in an appendix.

The discipline is not "we are reproducible." It is "here is exactly how reproduced these numbers are, and the answer today is: attested, not reproduced." That is a weaker marketing line and a stronger trust line. We took the trust line.

4. W6: we tried to reproduce, and published the failure

A claim of "the harness exists" is cheap until someone runs it. So we ran it. The W6 workstream (2026-05-21, branch feat/W6-factory-rerun-DC-000) re-derived all 23 parse-intent rows of section 5.1.1 through the pinned factory harness at commit 3382abb. The headline result:

QuantityValue
Cells attempted23 / 23
Green repro_check (factory re-derived)0 / 23
Red on structural impossibility23 / 23
Confidence intervals added0
Attested rows retracted0
reproduced_by_factory true-flips0

Zero of twenty-three. That is not a typo, and it is not a scandal — it is a boundary, named precisely. The W6 attestation records three structural causes, all logged per-cell:

  1. No adapters for vendor models in the SEED harness. The open SEED harness is offline by design: no API keys, no network egress, no vendor SDKs. Its adapter registry holds only stdlib-only seed adapters. The 23 hosted-API and third-party VLM rows have no adapter, so --model GPT-4o correctly exits with unknown model.
  2. No loader for the in-house corpus. The parse-intent-inhouse-v1 dataset is not in the repository — it was not licensed for redistribution as part of the 2026-05-18 attestation. Without the corpus, no per-document score can be computed.
  3. No per-document scores. The attested source is corpus-level aggregate. Bootstrap CIs need per-document resamples that do not exist.

The crucial detail: when the harness went red, nothing was patched to make it green. No attested number was quietly adjusted. No ci95 was filled with a plausible-looking interval. No reproduced_by_factory flag was flipped. The 23 attested F1/WER pairs were preserved exactly, the audit table was published next to the results, and a footnote was added to section 5.1.1. A failed reproduction attempt that you publish in full is worth more than a successful one nobody can see.

5. Enforcement lives in code, not in a promise

Discipline that depends on author goodwill decays. SAVIOR-Bench v1 moves the enforcement into the type system and the harness. The clearest example is the tabular-reasoning stream, TR-000. It is registered — it has a task entry, a metric spec (TEDS primary; GRITS and cell-F1 secondary), pinned decoding — but it is REGISTERED-NOT-YET-RUN. And harness/metrics.py score_item("TR-000") raises NotImplementedError by design; harness/tabular_metrics.py is a typed scaffold with NotImplementedError bodies.

The consequence is structural: there is no TR-000 number anywhere in the repository, so there is no TR-000 number that can accidentally land in a slide, a tweet, or a sales deck. You cannot leak a result that the code refuses to produce. The honest corpus ceiling for TR-000 is stated as ~3.5k–6.8k documents; a rounder, nicer "10k" is explicitly not asserted anywhere in the program.

The same principle governs confidence intervals. Every ci95 field is null, surfaced to readers as "reported (no CI)". A null interval is honest about uncertainty we have not measured. A fabricated ±0.01 would be a lie with a decimal point. The harness carries a per-cell rerun_command string on every published cell, so no reader can ever mistake an attested cell for a reproduced one.

6. Why the weaker claim is the stronger asset

It would have been easy to write "SAVIOR-Bench v1: a fully reproducible financial-document benchmark." It would also have been false the moment a reader tried repro_check.py against a vendor row. The no-faked-cell discipline accepts a less punchy sentence — "attested, with a named, audited path to reproduction" — because that sentence survives contact with a skeptic.

For anyone evaluating document-AI systems, the transferable practice is small and cheap: separate attested from reproduced in your own vocabulary, demand a per-cell provenance string, treat a null CI as more honest than a suspiciously tidy one, and ask any vendor to show you their failed reproduction attempts, not just their leaderboard. A benchmark that publishes its 0/23 is telling you something true. A benchmark with no audit at all is telling you nothing — confidently.

The No-Faked-Cell Discipline: Reporting Reproducibility a Benchmark Has Not Yet Earned

Hyperbots Research · SAVIOR-Bench Working Group · snapshot 2026-05-20
Abstract. We present the reproducibility methodology of SAVIOR-Bench v1, a 509-document expert-annotated financial-document benchmark over 21 OCR and 23 parse-intent systems. Rather than claiming reproducibility, the benchmark enforces a no-faked-cell discipline: cells that cannot be computed honestly raise errors, confidence intervals absent per-document scores are reported as null, and every published cell is labelled attested-not-reproduced with a per-cell rerun command. A factory re-reproduction audit (W6) of all 23 parse-intent cells re-derived 0/23 under the open harness, due to three named structural causes; the failure is published in full with zero retractions and zero fabricated intervals. We argue that an honest weaker claim is a stronger trust asset than an unverifiable stronger one.

1. Introduction

"Reproducible" is widely over-claimed in applied ML evaluation. SAVIOR-Bench v1 separates two states: attested (a named party ran and signed numbers on a date) and reproduced (a deterministic harness re-derived them under a pinned environment with a green check). The benchmark is currently the former and openly not the latter.

2. Method

Corpus: 509 financial documents over five corpora and six document classes; IAA 0.761 over 187 double-annotated pairs. Scoring: word-level multiset recall/precision (whole-token, CER rejected); PaIRS layout penalty (PC-only; NPC = format-compat proxy); parse-intent F1 and WER. Decoding pinned (temperature 0.1, top-p 0.9, max_tokens 2048, seed 20260516); one frozen prompt per task family. The tabular stream TR-000 is registered-not-yet-run: score_item("TR-000") raises NotImplementedError, so no tabular number exists in the repository. Confidence intervals are null where per-document scores are absent.

3. Results: the W6 audit

All 23 parse-intent cells were re-run through the pinned harness (commit 3382abb).

QuantityValue
Cells attempted23/23
Green repro_check0/23
Red (structural)23/23
CIs added0
Rows retracted0
true-flips0

Three structural causes: (i) no vendor adapters in the offline SEED harness; (ii) the in-house corpus is not redistributable; (iii) no per-document scores exist. Attested F1/WER pairs were preserved unchanged.

4. Limitations

All cells are attested, not factory-reproduced. No confidence intervals (ci95 = null). No per-class/per-quality/per-field slices. PaIRS is comparable only among prompt-controlled models. Single benchmark version (v1, 2026-05-18). Fine-tuned rows are LoRA fine-tunes on in-domain SAVIOR-Train data and are not apples-to-apples with zero-shot rows. Benchmark figures are model performance, not product accuracy.

5. Conclusion

Reproducibility is a status, not an adjective. Publishing a 0/23 audit and a named path to reproduction is a stronger trust position than an unverifiable claim of full reproducibility.
