Hyperbots Content / Benchmark / Experience / Tech-Moat Virality Strategy

technical-content-strategist + launch-pr-strategist (CTO-org) · 2026-05-22
The premise, stated once: the honest, non-obvious finding is the hook — not a hype number. Hyperbots already has the rare thing most AI companies fake: real measured artifacts, and the discipline to say what they could not establish. That discipline is the virality lever — it is what makes a technical audience trust the post enough to share it. A post that converts once and erodes trust is a net loss.

1. Positioning — the through-line per brand

Three brands, one spine: "Document AI for finance, built and measured in the open — including the parts that don't work yet."

Hyperbots (the company)

Hyperbots runs its own R&D, benchmarking, and go-to-market as agent systems in version control — and publishes what the agents could not establish, not just what they could.

Verifiable in the repos: ABM is 14 agents in one git repo; the benchmark factory is a 9-agent roster with a STOP-on-mismatch repro gate; the repo factory is a 13-agent pipeline behind 10,131 generated use-case repos. The proof point no competitor can cheaply copy is the negative finding shipped on purpose — the W6 audit that re-derived 0/23 attested cells and retracted nothing.

HyperAPI (the document-AI API product)

HyperAPI turns finance, bank, shipping, and custom documents into clean structured output — and the fine-tune edge that powers it is smaller than you'd assume, which is exactly why an API-native pipeline still wins in production.

The hook is the under-claim: the fine-tuned model leads the best documented zero-shot system by +0.0276 F1 on parse-intent and +0.0061 recall on OCR. A buyer sold "10x better" by every vendor stops scrolling at a vendor admitting the gap is small — then reads the real argument (latency, cost-per-correct at ≈$0.19/1,000 successful calls, the API surface, 10k+ use-case repos). Product accuracy only "per apis.hyperbots.com"; benchmark figures are model OCR/parse performance.

CFO-office AI expertise (the domain-authority play)

Hyperbots has mapped the finance back office — AP, AR, accruals, vendor management, collections, cash application, FP&A, lease accounting, chargebacks — into thousands of concrete named tasks, and can show the work on any one.

The repo factory's 10,131 repos, the Reddit listening agent's 13 solution areas, and the ICP's four document classes are a taxonomy of the CFO office nobody else has published. Content here teaches, not sells — it builds the audience that later converts on HyperAPI.

2. Tech-moat narrative — honest about what is and isn't defensible

Genuinely defensible (the moat, today)

  1. The benchmark factory as a process, not a number. SAVIOR-Bench (509 docs, 5 corpora, IAA 0.761, 23-system parse-intent panel) is strong; the defensible asset is the factory: a pinned deterministic stdlib-only harness, a contamination audit per (model, dataset), a STOP-on-mismatch repro gate, per-cell rerun commands. Competitors can copy a screenshot; not the discipline of a roster that blocks its own release on a reproducibility mismatch.
  2. The Hyperbots-IP fine-tuned models. Open-source backbones (Qwen2.5-VL-3B/7B) fine-tuned on SAVIOR-Train via LoRA — Hyperbots IP. The moat is the in-domain financial training corpus and the improvement loop, not the narrow per-task edge.
  3. The 10k+ use-case repo corpus. 10,131 generated repos (2,341 tracked Tier-1 primitives) — a distribution surface, onboarding accelerator, and taxonomy moat at once.
  4. The agent-built GTM and R&D. ABM (14), benchmark factory (9), repo factory (13), Reddit listening, the autonomous ML research loop — operating leverage a contractor-and-SaaS competitor cannot match.

Honest about what is NOT yet a moat

  • not a moat SAVIOR-Bench is attested, not factory-reproduced. Headline numbers were run in-house, user-attested 2026-05-18; the W6 audit re-derived 0/23 DC-000 cells by structural design. Content must never claim the headline numbers are independently reproducible — only the 12-cell offline SEED leaderboard is.
  • not a moat No third-party audit, no external validation, no CIs on the headline table.
  • not a moat No customer-outcome data. The ABM engine has sent zero messages — no engagement, reply, or revenue metrics exist.
  • disclosed The fine-tune edge is narrow — stated as the hook, not hidden.

4. Per-channel plan

Cadence is sustainable. "Virality pursued credibly" = honest finding leads, one truthful chart, distribution to the right technical communities rather than bought reach, no astroturf ever.

GitHub — for the developer who will actually try it

Cadence: continuous (source of truth); a tagged "drop" monthly. Format: public repos, honest READMEs, ≤6-line quickstarts, SEED leaderboard, every doc ends with ## Sources + ## Open assumptions.

First 3: (1) Publish the benchmark factory repo with the SEED leaderboard and W6 audit visible — the audit is a feature. (2) A curated 25-repo "finance document AI cookbook" from the repo factory. (3) A "reproduce our SEED cells" README — the one place where "re-run on your laptop" is literally true.

Credible virality: stars come from a repo that runs in one command and admits its limits; the W6 audit in-repo is the share-bait for a crowd tired of irreproducible leaderboards.

LinkedIn — for founders, GTM leaders, finance operators

Cadence: 3 posts/week (1 founder, 1 research, 1 CFO-office teach). Format: short narrative, one honest chart or number, ABM persona depth (CFO, VP Finance, Controller, Procurement, IT/CIO, ERP Lead).

First 3: (1) Founder post from "We Built Our Entire ABM Engine in Claude Code" — "zero messages sent" up front. (2) Research post: the +0.0276 F1 small-gap finding. (3) CFO-office teach: "We mapped the finance back office into [N] document tasks — here are 10 nobody automates."

Credible virality: "we haven't sent a message yet" and "the gap is small" out-perform hype — they signal honesty to a skeptical feed.

Blog / website (owned) — the durable canonical home

Cadence: 2 long-form posts/month, the canonical source other channels link back to. Format: Tier-1 technical essays, one honest chart, explicit Sources/Open-assumptions, Hyperbots light theme.

First 3: (1) Polish + publish "Our Agents train, benchmark and deploy while we sleep" — resolve the {{COST_FIGURE}} placeholder or cut the "$20" framing first. (2) "SAVIOR-Bench: the honest version" — the small gap, attested-not-reproduced status, W6 audit, all in the lead. (3) "What 10,000 finance-document tasks taught us about the CFO office."

Credible virality: slow, compounding — these become the link every thread and HN comment points to; honesty makes them quotable by third parties.

Twitter / X — for the document-AI + ML research crowd

Cadence: 4-5 posts/week; one thread per major artifact. Format: the honest-hook thread (surprising-but-true line, one chart, link to canonical blog). Reuse the HN/X variants in launch/POST.md.

First 3: (1) The small-gap thread — "we fine-tuned an open model... the edge over the best zero-shot model was +0.0061 recall. The interesting result isn't the leaderboard." (2) The W6 audit thread — "we built a harness to reproduce our own benchmark. It reproduced 0 of 23 cells. Here's why that's the most useful thing it did." (3) A cost thread — measured A100 economics, ≈$0.19/1,000 successful calls.

Credible virality: X rewards the contrarian-but-true take; "our benchmark failed to reproduce and we published it anyway" is genuinely novel. No engagement bought; no sock-puppets.

Webinars — for the buying committee, mid-funnel

Cadence: 1/month, ~30 min + Q&A. Format: live walk-through of a real artifact, not slideware.

First 3: (1) "How we benchmark document AI — live" — run the SEED leaderboard, show the repro-gate STOP. (2) "The agent-built GTM" — walk the 14-agent ABM repo and the PR/QA loop. (3) "Finance-document AI, task by task" — 5 repo-factory tasks live.

Credible virality: the recording is the asset; clipped honest moments ("watch the harness STOP its own release") seed other channels.

Podcasts — reach into adjacent founder/finance audiences

Cadence: 1-2 guest appearances/month; optionally a short owned series in Q2. Format: founder/research-lead as guest; the narrative is the three honest claims, never a feature dump.

First 3 (pitches): (1) AI-engineering podcast — "we ran a benchmark audit that failed and shipped it." (2) CFO/finance-ops podcast — "the CFO office as a task map." (3) Startup/GTM podcast — "we built our whole ABM motion as 14 agents in git."

Credible virality: podcasts convert on story; the agent-built-company story is concrete and verifiable. Always disclose: drafts, gates, no metrics claimed.

5. Benchmark & experience content engine

  • Every leaderboard refresh = a content event — new model/task/re-run cell becomes a short post; the per-cell rerun command keeps each refresh checkable.
  • Every negative finding = a flagship post — the W6 0/23 audit is the template; the most differentiating content move available, costing nothing but honesty.
  • Experience content from real usage — once it exists. Today there is no customer-outcome data and the ABM engine has sent zero messages; the engine runs on internal build experience for now. Real product usage graduates into case studies — cited "per apis.hyperbots.com," never fabricated.
  • The repo factory as an evergreen mill — each Tier-1 primitive (parse/split/classify/detect/extract/validate/reconcile) is a micro-post.
  • Reddit listening as a content radar — the daily themed digest surfaces what the finance community actually asks; recurring questions become blog topics. The agent never posts; humans may reply manually with disclosure.

7. Measurement — "viral but credible"

Leading indicators (credibility-first):

  • GitHub: stars, forks, and external "I re-ran your SEED cells" confirmations — the credibility metric that matters most.
  • Blog: time-on-page, scroll depth, inbound third-party citations.
  • X/LinkedIn: saves and qualified replies > raw likes; reshares by named practitioners > anonymous reshares.
  • Webinar: live attendance, Q&A depth, recording completion. Podcast: referral traffic to the canonical blog.

What "viral but credible" looks like: the honest finding (small gap; 0/23 audit) is what gets quoted; reach from practitioner reshares and organic community pickup, not paid amplification or astroturf; zero corrections forced after publish; inbound interest references the method ("you publish your failures").

Anti-goals: a one-time spike with no citations; engagement on a claim we cannot defend; growth attributable to undisclosed community activity.
