Launching our parse testbench: 28 vendors, 4 doctypes, real gold

A vendor's PDF tells you what their system does on their documents, under their evaluation conditions. It does not tell you what it does on yours.

That asymmetry is why we built a parse testbench. The bench covers 28 vendors across three measurement tracks — parse-intent F1, OCR edit-distance, and multi-corpus parse success rate — against three datasets with documented gold provenance. Every number on the leaderboard traces to an on-disk cell file. Cells that are PENDING-KEY or PENDING-CORPUS are honest nulls, not hidden rows.

This post describes what we measure, what we have on disk today, and what's pending.

Why a testbench

Vendor benchmarks have a structural problem: the team shipping the product chooses the benchmark. Extend's RealDocBench is a serious effort — 1,500 human-annotated samples for layout accuracy, 1,359 prompts for Q&A accuracy, PII replaced with synthetic values — and we respect the rigor. But it was built to showcase Extend Parse 2.0 on the document types Extend handles well: IRS forms, SEC reports, mortgage loan documents, logistics/supply chain.

Our document types are different: invoices, bank statements, cheques, credit notes, remittance advice. Our failure modes are different: MICR encoding on cheque images, multi-currency running-balance tables on bank statements, polarity-reversed fields on credit notes that look structurally identical to invoices. And our question is different: on these five types, across these 28+ vendors, which systems produce accurate structured output, and by how much?

A bench you run yourself, on your own gold, against the vendors you actually care about, answers that question. A vendor PDF does not.

What we measure

Track 1 — parse-intent F1 (SAVIOR-Bench v1, 28+ vendors)

Parse-intent is the task of extracting structured fields from a document according to an explicit intent schema — e.g., "return all line items with quantity, unit price, and tax code." The metric is standard token-level F1 between predicted and gold field values.

The leaderboard covers 28 vendors including VLMs, specialist OCR systems, and general-purpose language models. All 24 cells with metric values are in-house-attested (source: benchmark.xlsx, 2026-05-18). They are not factory-harness-reproduced (SEED harness lacks vendor adapters; all factory reruns returned unknown model). Corpus is not on disk; n is null across all cells; no confidence intervals are reported.

Source path pattern: results/DC-000__parse-intent-inhouse-v1__<vendor>.json

Track 2 — OCR edit-distance (SAVIOR-Bench v1, 31 systems)

OCR edit-distance is measured as normalized Levenshtein distance between predicted and gold transcription (range 0–1, lower is better). The leaderboard covers 31 systems spanning pipeline tools, specialist VLMs, and general VLMs.

All 31 OCR cells are externally-sourced attested — transcribed verbatim from the SAVIOR-Bench OCR leaderboard table (/tmp/savior-ocr/ocr-edit-distance.tsv, user-provided 2026-05-22). They were not run by the factory harness. Factory reproduction would require the underlying SAVIOR OCR corpus, which is not on disk. No confidence intervals are reported.

The HyperAPI-PaddleOCR-OURS pipeline sits at rank 12 / 31+ entries with edit-distance 0.061 — a 36% relative reduction vs. Tesseract OCR (0.096) and a 14% relative reduction vs. open-source PaddleOCR-v4 (0.071).

Source: results/DC-000__ocr-edit-distance-v1__HyperAPI-PaddleOCR-OURS.json

Datasets

Multi-corpus (5 corpora, 2,649 docs)

A five-corpus sweep of finance documents: results (invoices), results-converted (format-converted invoices), tough (all four tough doctypes), parsing (full multi-page docs and extract samples), and soulshine2 (remittance and statement samples). Documents span PDF, PNG, TIFF, and converted-from-original formats. Long documents (≥50 pages) are chunked at 25-page windows and counted honestly — no silent drops.

Tough-bench (174 docs, per-doc gold meta.json)

Expert-annotated gold labels across four doctypes: invoice, bank_statement, cheque, credit_note, remittance_advice. Each document has a meta.json with classifier_type, named entities, and line-item fields. Scoring is done by the factory harness (score_tough_jsonls.py) against meta.json. No customer names are used; any real-world documents are anonymized or sourced from public archives.

SAVIOR-Bench v1 (509 docs, attested)

An externally-produced financial-document benchmark with three measurement facets: extraction (parse-intent F1), layout awareness, and bounding box. Inter-annotator agreement (IAA) on the gold is 0.761. Of the three facets, only the extraction facet (parse-intent F1 + OCR edit-distance) is measured in our current leaderboard; the layout-awareness and bounding-box facets are defined but not yet measured by us.

What's anchored to disk

Every published number traces to a cell file. The path pattern is:

results/DC-{task_id}__{dataset_name}__{vendor_slug}.json

Each cell file contains: task, dataset, vendor, metric_name, metric_value, n, provenance, factory_reproduced (true/false), and notes. A cell with factory_reproduced=false means the value was attested from an external source or in-house benchmark run, not reproduced by the factory harness.

Example cells:

  • results/DC-000__parse-intent-inhouse-v1__hyperapi-parse-intent.json — F1 0.8328, in-house-attested, factory_reproduced=false
  • results/DC-000__ocr-edit-distance-v1__HyperAPI-PaddleOCR-OURS.json — edit-distance 0.061, externally_sourced=true, factory_reproduced=false
  • results/DC-006__multicorpus__hyperapi-parse.json — 2,612/2,612 parse success, factory_reproduced=true
  • results/DC-011__tough-cheque__hyperapi-classify.json — 21/21 classify accuracy, Wilson CI95, factory_reproduced=true

The layout IoU task is the most significant structural gap relative to Extend's RealDocBench. Their primary metric — Adjusted F1 on bounding-box region detection across 1,500 human-annotated samples — measures something fundamentally different from parse-intent F1. These two metrics are not directly comparable and we do not present them side-by-side. Building a layout IoU benchmark would require a document corpus with bounding-box ground truth and an IoU scoring harness — currently estimated at 2–3 weeks of effort plus the PENDING-KEY providers.

How to reproduce

The factory harness lives in hyperapi-documentapi-benchmark-factory/harness/. Three entry points cover the three attested tracks:

# OCR edit-distance — ingest from externally-sourced TSV
python harness/ingest_ocr_edit_distance.py \
  --input /tmp/savior-ocr/ocr-edit-distance.tsv \
  --output results/DC-000__ocr-edit-distance-v1__

# Parse-intent — ingest from benchmark.xlsx (in-house-attested)
python harness/ingest_savior_xlsx.py \
  --input benchmark.xlsx \
  --task parse_intent_f1 \
  --output results/DC-000__parse-intent-inhouse-v1__

# Multi-corpus parse sweep — run against HyperAPI live
python harness/run_multicorpus_parse.py \
  --corpora results results-converted tough parsing soulshine2 \
  --output results/DC-006__multicorpus__hyperapi-parse.json

# Tough-bench classify sweep
python harness/run_tough_classify.py \
  --doctypes cheque credit_note remittance_advice \
  --gold-dir tough-bench/meta/ \
  --output-prefix results/DC-011__tough-

All harness scripts require hyperapi_test_key in realqa/secrets/. Factory-reproduced cells write factory_reproduced=true in the output JSON. Attested cells from external sources write factory_reproduced=false and preserve the source provenance string.

Methodology notes

  • All benchmark numbers are model-level research metrics, not product accuracy. Product accuracy is at apis.hyperbots.com.
  • The parse-intent leaderboard (24 rows with values) is in-house-attested and not factory-harness-reproduced. n is null across all cells; no confidence intervals are available.
  • The OCR edit-distance leaderboard (31 rows) is externally-sourced attested; source table provides point estimates only; no CI.
  • The multi-corpus parse sweep (DC-006, factory_reproduced=true) used preproduction SDK 0fc4bada; production behavior may differ.
  • Tough-bench classify sweep (DC-011, factory_reproduced=true) was scored against Hyperbots-curated gold labels; Wilson CI95 reported.
  • No customer names appear in this post. Documents in the corpus are anonymized or sourced from public archives.
  • results.zip (Hyperbots deployed-IDP output) was not used as ground truth for any competitive comparison. It appears in the multi-corpus sweep only as a source of runnable documents for parse-success measurement.
