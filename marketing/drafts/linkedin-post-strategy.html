§1–§5 is justified against one or more of these.

1. Positioning — the LinkedIn through-line

The cross-channel spine is set in the virality strategy: *"Document AI for

finance, built and measured in the open — including the parts that don't

work yet."* LinkedIn's job is the founder + finance-operator slice of

that spine. The platform-specific positioning:

The honest claim for LinkedIn: *Hyperbots builds, benchmarks and runs
its go-to-market as agent systems in version control — and on this feed we
show the work, including the negative findings, because a skeptical
professional audience shares what it trusts, not what it is sold.*

Three things make this work on LinkedIn specifically, and none of them is a

metric we do not have:

1. The honest finding is the scroll-stopper. A finance or ML

professional has been told "10x" by every vendor in their feed. A founder

saying "the fine-tune edge over zero-shot was smaller than we expected —

here is the real number and why API-native still wins" is genuinely

novel. Per the virality strategy, the parse-intent edge is +0.0276 F1 and

the OCR edge +0.0061 recall — disclosed as the hook, not buried.

Why shareable: S6 (quotable first line) + S7 (negative-finding novelty)

+ S8 (specificity) — the three highest-leverage signals stacked.

Earn Trust check: Adds value even if the reader has no intent to

buy — they learn something real about the category. ✓

2. The build story is concrete and verifiable. The 14-agent ABM engine,

the 9-agent benchmark factory, the repo-factory corpus — these are

checkable in version control. Concreteness beats adjectives on this feed.

Why shareable: S4 (named-practitioner reshare — engineers reshare

provably-real builds) + S5 (compounding reputation: "they publish their

stack"). Earn Trust check: A reader could clone the repo and verify

today; nothing requires belief. ✓

3. The CFO-office teach line earns the audience before it sells. Mapping

finance-document work task by task is teaching content; it builds the

operator following that later evaluates HyperAPI.

Why shareable: S1 (CFO-office readers save task-map posts for

reference) + S10 (a controller's 2nd-degree shares to their AP team).

Earn Trust check: Pure teaching — the task map is useful whether

the reader ever buys. ✓

Guardrail recap, in force for every post in this document: no named

competitor (category labels — "OCR-plus-rules", "legacy IDP" — only);

product accuracy only "per apis.hyperbots.com"; benchmark figures labelled

model OCR/parse performance; models = open-source backbones fine-tuned by

Hyperbots ("Hyperbots IP"); pricing only the published $0.05/page,

volume sized on a call; no HITL/manual-review framing; no fabricated

metrics or follower counts; no customer-outcome claim (the ABM engine has

sent zero messages).

All posts are DRAFT and pass the human gate. Weeks are 2026.

(LinkedIn-ranking-signal mapped) and one *Earn Trust* line (passes the

that fails either gets rewritten or dropped; it never gets boosted to

compensate.

4. Engagement tactics

Engagement is part of the strategy, not an afterthought — but every tactic

below is organic and honest. No bought engagement, no pods, no star-for-star,

no sock-puppets. **Each tactic is mapped to the specific LinkedIn ranking

signal it targets**; tactics without a signal-mapping are not on this list.

  • Per-post engagement action, mirroring the ABM generator's pattern:

- *Founder/POV posts (Mon):* the author comments substantively on 3

finance-AI or CFO-office posts the same day — adding a real point, never

"great post."

- *Research/proof posts (Wed):* reshare or quote-comment 1 credible

practitioner or partner artifact, with attribution.

- *Teach posts (Fri):* reply in-thread to every qualified question; route

deeper ones to the canonical blog or a runnable repo.

- Why this works: S2 (substantive comment depth lifts the *commented-on*

posts AND signals to LinkedIn that the author is part of a real

conversation, which lifts the author's *own* future posts) + S4

(reshare-with-attribution is the lowest-cost way to earn a reciprocal

named reshare later). Earn Trust: every comment passes "would this add

value if I weren't a vendor?" — none of them say "great post; check out

our blog." ✓

  • Comment, don't broadcast. The author replies to every substantive

comment within the first 24 hours — LinkedIn's ranking rewards early

author replies, and a real conversation is the honest version of "boosting

reach."

- Why this works: S3 (early author reply within 60 min is empirically

one of the strongest distribution signals on the platform; 24h cap is the

backstop). Earn Trust: replies are substantive — they don't try to

"neg" a critical comment into engagement. ✓

  • Disclosure is mandatory. Any team member engaging with Hyperbots

content does so from their real identity and discloses the affiliation

("I work on Hyperbots") — never an anonymous assist.

- Why this works: S4 (a known-affiliated comment is honest; an

undisclosed-affiliate comment is sock-puppeting, which violates the

honesty bar in §6 and is also detectable, which would cost S5 forever).

Earn Trust: disclosure is the bar — this isn't a tactic, it's a

floor we never cross. ✓

  • Cross-link, don't repost. A LinkedIn post links to the canonical blog

or repo; it does not paste the whole thing. The blog is the durable home;

LinkedIn is the doorway.

- Why this works: S1 (a post that *promises* a useful artifact

earns the save; an inline-everything post earns a scroll). Earn Trust:

the link goes to a *real* artifact, not a gated email-capture form. ✓

  • Persona-paced asks. The CTA matches persona tier: operators get a soft

"where does this break for you?"; an evaluator-leaning post may offer the

runnable repo. Pricing, if it ever appears, is only the published

$0.05/page with "volume sized on a call" — never an invented quote.

- Why this works: S2 (an open question CTA outperforms a "DM me"

CTA by a wide margin because it invites the comment, which is the

ranking signal). Earn Trust: no CTA promises a discount, a

"limited time offer," or anything not real. ✓

  • The break-even rule. If a post needs a hyped line to perform, the post

is wrong — rewrite the post, do not buy the reach.

- Why this works: S5 (the moment we hype to compensate, we forfeit

the durable "they publish honestly" reputation; one bought line costs

a year of S5). Earn Trust: this is the core of the honesty bar —

no hype, no boost, ever. ✓

Anti-tactics we explicitly reject (and why each fails the LinkedIn

ranking math, separately from failing the honesty bar):

  • Engagement pods / pod-rings: LinkedIn's anti-spam team detects

cluster-engagement patterns; even when not detected, pod likes don't

produce reshares-by-name (S4) or quoted-by-name placement (S5), so they

buy nothing durable. Also banned by §6.

  • "Hot take" without an anchor: fails S8 (specificity) and burns S5

(compounding credibility) once readers notice the pattern. We don't

publish hot takes; we publish honest findings.

  • "Excited to announce" launch posts: fails S6 (not quotable) and S7

(no negative-finding novelty). We announce launches inside a

surprising-but-true frame, or we don't post.

  • Follow-bait ("Comment 'X' for the link"): fails the Earn Trust

gate AND violates the §6 honesty bar (engagement-manipulation).

5. Credible-virality plan

The premise, inherited verbatim from the virality strategy and the

technical-content-strategist mandate: **the honest, non-obvious finding is

the hook.** On LinkedIn this is not a constraint we tolerate — it is the

single highest-leverage move available, because a professional feed is

saturated with hype and starved of candor.

The three honest findings that lead this calendar (each mapped to its

ranking-signal target so we know what we're optimizing for):

1. "The fine-tune edge is smaller than you'd think." Parse-intent

+0.0276 F1; OCR +0.0061 recall. A vendor saying this stops the scroll of

a buyer who has been sold "10x" all week — and then earns the read of the

real argument (API-native pipeline, measured economics, the corpus moat).

Target signals: S6 (the +0.0276 line is screenshot-quotable) + S7

(negative-finding novelty) + S8 (4-decimal specificity). Earn Trust: an

ML practitioner gets a real argument; a finance buyer learns the gap is

smaller than the market narrative. ✓

2. **"Our benchmark harness reproduced 0 of 23 cells — and we published

it."** The W6 audit. This is genuinely novel: most companies bury a

negative finding; shipping it on purpose is the share-bait for a

skeptical technical crowd. It is shareable *because* it is true.

Target signals: S5 (the compounding "they publish failures"

reputation begins here) + S7 (negative-finding) + S4 (engineers reshare

verifiable-rare honesty). Earn Trust: the rare vendor publishing its

own audit gives the field a real data point. ✓

3. **"We built our GTM as 14 agents in git — and it has sent zero

messages."** The build story with the deflating fact stated up front.

Honesty about what has *not* happened yet is itself the credibility

signal. Target signals: S6 (the "zero" hook is the post in one line)

+ S4 (founder builders reshare verifiable-real architectures) + S9

(named-account founder voice). Earn Trust: zero metric inflated;

the post earns more from "zero" than any positive number could. ✓

What "viral but credible" looks like here — and what it explicitly is not:

  • Working: saves and qualified replies outrank raw likes; reshares by

named practitioners outrank reshares by anonymous accounts; the honest

finding (small gap; 0/23 audit) is the line that gets quoted. *Mapped:*

S1, S2, S4, S5, S6.

  • Not working / anti-goals: a one-time spike with no qualified

conversation; engagement on a claim we cannot defend; any reach

attributable to undisclosed activity. No follower-count or vanity-metric

target is set, because none would be honest to chase. *Mapped:* none of

these correspond to durable ranking signals — they're noise.

  • Zero forced corrections. Every claim shipped was attested or

reproduced first; nothing in this calendar requires a retraction later.

*Mapped:* S5 (a forced correction is a year's worth of S5 erased in

one post; the only safe path is to ship only what's verified).

The compounding asset is the *method* — "they publish their failures." That

reputation is what makes a third party quote a Hyperbots post, and a quoted

post is worth more than a boosted one. Mapped: S5 is the asset we are

literally building post by post. Every L1 (honest benchmark) and L3

(agent-built) post is a deposit; every avoided hype-line is a non-withdrawal.

6. The honesty bar — the explicit "we will NOT do" list

Verbatim from the gtm-b2c-virality skill, scoped to LinkedIn. This is the

floor; nothing in this calendar is permitted to brush it.

  • No sock-puppets. Every Hyperbots-affiliated comment discloses

affiliation in line 1 (see §8 disclosure templates).

  • No upvote rings, no bought engagement, no engagement pods. Ever.

Includes "please like and reshare" asks to the team, family, or friends

with the intent of gaming early-window ranking.

  • No coordinated brigade. Don't ask the team to upvote-and-comment to

game ranking. (Substantive engagement from disclosed-affiliate

colleagues is fine — and per §4 above, expected — but it's

conversation, not a brigade.)

  • No fabricated metrics. No follower count, no engagement number, no

customer-outcome claim. The ABM engine has sent zero messages and the

partner-PII gate v1 is empty — both stay true on every post.

  • No named-competitor disparagement. Category labels only

("OCR-plus-rules", "legacy IDP", "rules-and-templates IDP"). The named

comparison stays inside the governed benchmark artifact.

  • No "regular user happens to know about this product" framing. We

don't pretend to be a third party.

  • No DMs that pretend to be helpful but are pitches. If outreach is a

pitch, the first line says so.

  • No covering up failures. "Below target" is stated plainly. The

6-week retro post (#16) is the canonical example; the principle holds

for every campaign.

  • No follow-bait or comment-gated rewards ("Comment 'X' for the

link"). The link goes in the post.

  • No "thanks for the kind words" comments. Reply substantively or

don't reply.

A draft that brushes any line here gets rewritten or dropped. It does not

get boosted to compensate.

7. Pre-post Earn Trust checklist (every draft must pass)

Before a post leaves the human gate, the author runs this checklist. A

single No is sufficient to send the draft back.

1. Earn Trust gate: Would this post add value if I weren't a vendor?

*(The single most important check. Per the skill: "the credibility we

build by saying-no on weak posts is what makes the rare strong post

work.")*

2. Surprising-but-true: Is the central claim something a reasonable

informed reader would not have predicted? *(If it's predictable, S7

doesn't fire; rewrite or drop.)*

3. Anchor present: Does every quantitative claim cite a named artifact

9. Honest funnel measurement (UTM + 7-day retro)

Per the gtm-b2c-virality skill — every link in a LinkedIn post is

tracked, every campaign gets a 7-day retro, and below-target results are

reported plainly.

  • UTM template: every outbound link carries

?utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=organic&utm_campaign=<post-id>&utm_content=<hook-label>.

Post-IDs come from the calendar in §3 (week1-mon-may26, etc.).

  • Signup tracking: the apis.hyperbots.com path. No inflation, no

attribution magic — a click that reaches a signup is a signup; a click

that doesn't, isn't.

  • 7-day post-campaign retrospective: impressions / clicks / saves /

comments / signups / what we'd do differently. Goes to

~/Desktop/hyperapi-abm-team/content/gtm-b2c/funnel.md (or, while that

path is being stood up, linkedin/funnel-retros/<post-id>.md in this

team).

  • Below-target plainly: if a post under-performed, the retro says

exactly that. The credibility of the next campaign depends on honesty

about this one. Per the skill: *"Below-target results are reported

plainly. The credibility of next campaign depends on honesty about this

one."*

  • No vanity-metric reporting: we do not report follower count,

total-likes, or impressions-without-context. The reportable signals

are: save-rate (proxy for S1), comment-depth (proxy for S2),

named-practitioner-reshares (S4), and signups (the only commercial

outcome that's both honest and ours).

10. Definition of viral — and what isn't

What we count:

  • A post is "working" when it drives ≥1 named-practitioner reshare

with substantive added comment AND ≥3 saves AND a substantive comment

thread the author engaged in within 60 minutes.

  • A campaign is "working" when the 6-week trend in save-rate

outpaces the trend in like-rate. *(Save-rate is the durable engagement

signal; like-rate is the noise floor.)*

  • The compounding asset is "growing" when the count of third-party

posts that quote a Hyperbots post by name (S5) is non-zero and rising

quarter-over-quarter.

What we explicitly do not count:

  • Follower count (vanity; no follower-count target is set anywhere in

this calendar — see §6).

  • Total impressions (gameable; no quality signal).
  • Likes from undisclosed accounts (often pod-fed).
  • A one-time spike with no qualified conversation — that is *noise*, not

virality. If the spike doesn't produce ≥1 named-practitioner reshare

with substance, it isn't a hit.

Honest framing, from the skill: *"'Virality' here is reach-dependent and

never guaranteed; the deliverable is a credible artifact + a discovery + a

draft + honest measurement."* This calendar produces the credible

artifact, the discovery, and the draft; reach is what the audience does

with it. We do not chase the reach.
