A document parser that lives inside an LLM stack is not the same product as one that lives inside the CFO's office

A category quietly bifurcated. Most haven't noticed.

One side: the LLM-orchestration-stack document parser — front door of an LLM/RAG pipeline. PDF in, markdown chunks out, vector DB next. Tiered fast/accurate/multimodal modes, page-credit pricing, pre-wired into a developer framework.

Other side: API-native, finance-fine-tuned document AI — primitive-resolved endpoints whose outputs are typed records. Seven primitives — parse · classify · split · match · validate · augment · reason. Open-source backbones fine-tuned by Hyperbots (Hyperbots IP). $0.05 / page.

Both are excellent at what they're for. They are not for the same buyer.

Output shape decides. Markdown chunks are right for retrieval — chat-over-PDFs, contract-Q&A, knowledge-base. Typed records are right for posting an approved invoice to a GL with audit trail. You can't post a markdown chunk to a GL.

The seven-primitive gap. A RAG-pipeline-native parsing API owns one — parse. The other six are the integrator's problem. Right factoring for a developer; wrong factoring for a finance buyer paying to outsource assembly. P2P money lives in match; O2C money lives in reason. Neither is a parser problem.

Fine-tune posture, plainly. Every "AI for finance" post claims 10x. Honest version, per SAVIOR-Bench v1: the fine-tune edge over the best well-documented zero-shot is +0.0276 F1 on parse-intent (23 systems) and +0.0061 recall on OCR (21 systems). Real, but narrow — and it lives on the finance long tail (delivery dockets, bilingual carriers, multi-leg BoLs, spanning-header invoices). The argument needs the right output shape — not a blowout.

Benchmark posture. SAVIOR-Bench v1 — 509 docs, IAA 0.761, ci95=null (published as such). SEED leaderboard — 12 cells, reproducible on a laptop. W6 audit re-derived 0 of 23 cells. We published that.

Three questions decide buyer-fit.

1. Who pays — IT/AI engineering or the CFO's office?

2. What ships — markdown chunks or typed records?

3. Does the workload need the other six primitives out of the box?

Answer "CFO / typed records / yes" and you're not in the RAG-parser category. You're in a different one — and that's the distinction most "AI for finance" pitches blur.

The take. Stop ranking vendors. Start matching buyers. Category fit, not category superiority.

Challenge me. If you've shipped document AI for finance, which primitive broke first when you composed around a general-purpose parser — match, validate, or reason?
