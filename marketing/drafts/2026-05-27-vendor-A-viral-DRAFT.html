We ran a deep head-to-head against a category-leading IDP vendor — and the gap wasn't in the accuracy number

We just finished a deep head-to-head study against a category-leading IDP vendor — a trainable-IDP-with-an-API-veneer shop. The honest finding: on the published accuracy panel, our fine-tune edge over the strongest documented zero-shot system is +0.0276 F1. Real, but narrow.

That sentence got the read. Now the actual finding: the gap between us and a trainable-IDP-with-an-API-veneer stack isn't accuracy. It's on five other axes a finance buyer evaluates.

1. Backbone disclosure. Ours: open-source backbones, fine-tuned by Hyperbots (Hyperbots IP). LoRA recipe is in the paper, every fine-tuned row tagged [FT] inline. Theirs: closed stack.

2. API decomposition. Ours: primitive-resolved — parse, parse-intent, split, classify, extract, validate, reconcile, and more. Each is its own endpoint, its own price line, its own failure mode. Theirs: end-to-end "extraction." One box.

3. Published benchmark surface. Ours: SAVIOR-Bench v1, 509 docs, IAA 0.761, 23-system parse-intent panel. ci95=null published honestly. Our W6 audit re-derived 0 of 23 SAVIOR cells under our own harness — published. Theirs: pending verification.

4. Reproducibility. Ours: a 12-cell SEED leaderboard that runs on a laptop; a consolidated view with 160 rows, 16 factory-reproduced, 79 attested, per-row provenance. Theirs: pending verification.

5. Pricing. Ours: $0.05/page published, volume sized on a call. Theirs: pending verification.

The negative finding on our side, up front. On dataset_95_granular (95 invoices, factory-reproduced), our extract chain lands at 0.3573 micro-F1 — *below* Gemini 2.5 Flash (0.7132), Pro (0.6195), Azure DI (0.6078), and Flash-Lite (0.5004). Two long invoices hit the 300s extract timeout. We published that on 2026-05-25 and we're working it.

I'm not naming the vendor here. A head-to-head that names the competitor is marketing; one that names the *approach* is teaching. The named comparison lives in our internal gated study.

The take: the gap isn't accuracy — it's disclosure discipline. We publish the spine the other side, on the surface I've inspected, doesn't. That doesn't make us the better product for every buyer. It makes us the *verifiable* one.

If you've evaluated a trainable-IDP-with-an-API-veneer stack against an API-native fine-tuned stack — where did your evaluation break: the accuracy number, the primitive decomposition, the reproducibility, or the pricing?
