P-1 · 500 line items on one invoice — extracted accurately

Depth 6/10 · Audience: AP leaders, finance-ops engineers · DRAFT (pointers)

Angle

  • High-line-count invoices (telecom, distribution, parts catalogs) are where most doc-AI quietly degrades — truncation, table-continuation drift, OCR error accumulation. HyperAPI holds accuracy at the long tail.

Section pointers

  • Hook — "your extractor was demo'd on a 5-line invoice; production sends 500."
  • Why high-line-count invoices are hard — context limits, multi-page table continuation, per-line OCR drift, sub-totals vs grand-totals.
  • What "accurate at 500 lines" must mean — per-line-item precision/recall, not just header fields; line-item-count accuracy.
  • How HyperAPI handles it — page-spanning table assembly, line-item alignment, validation.
  • Evidence — a real ≥500-line invoice case with measured per-line accuracy.
⚠ Needs: a real ≥500-line example + measured accuracy. On disk today: dataset_95 has a 122-line Fastenal invoice — not yet 500. "500" and "accurately" cannot ship without a measured case.

P-4 · 9,000 payment-term types — and why applying the right discount is so hard

Depth 6/10 · Audience: AP leaders, controllers, finance-ops · DRAFT (pointers)

Angle

  • "2/10 Net 30" is the tip of the iceberg — real-world payment terms sprawl into thousands of variants, and that sprawl is why early-pay discount capture leaks money.

Section pointers

  • The combinatorial explosion — net days, discount %, discount windows, EOM/proximo, tiered, conditional, multi-currency.
  • Why it breaks discount capture — missed early-pay discounts = measurable lost margin; manual term interpretation doesn't scale.
  • How HyperAPI solves it — parse → normalize payment terms into a canonical structure → apply correct discount logic.
⚠ Needs: a defensible source/derivation for the "9,000" figure (combinatorial count? observed corpus?) — sourced or reframed to "thousands of variants." No invented count. Plus real capability detail.

P-5 · The account-number mismatch problem

Depth 5/10 · Audience: AP leaders, controllers · DRAFT (pointers)

Angle

  • A concrete, recognizable AP failure — the account number on the invoice doesn't match the one in the vendor master / GL / system of record.

Section pointers

  • What the mismatch is — invoice account # vs vendor-master / customer-record / GL account #.
  • Why it happens — formatting variance, OCR confusions (O↔0, l↔1), multiple account numbers per vendor, branch/division-level accounts, vendor re-numbering.
  • The downstream cost — misapplied payments, reconciliation effort, vendor disputes, aged items.
  • How HyperAPI detects + resolves it — field extraction + cross-reference/validation against the vendor master, anomaly flagging.
⚠ Lowest claim-risk of the five (a problem/solution explainer). Still needs real product-capability detail; no invented metrics.

Cross-cutting

  • Data dependencies: P-1 (500-line case), P-2 (0.5s + customer consent), P-3 (Azure study real numbers), P-4 ("9,000" source) all block on verified data. P-5 needs product detail only.
  • Review gates: P-2 (customer-named) and P-3 (competitor-named) require legal review + sign-off. None publishes without the human gate.
