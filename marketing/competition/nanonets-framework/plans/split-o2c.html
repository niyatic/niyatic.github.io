Split (O2C) — deep-study plan

1. Executive summary (TEMPLATE — numbers placeholder)

Multi-document O2C bundles arrive most often through lockbox intake (a TIFF/PDF containing many remittance advices, each bound to one or more check images) and through customer-portal exports (statement of account + a stapled set of remittance advices). How correctly does an IDP system split the bundle into its logical sub-documents? The remittance↔check pairing makes O2C splitting distinctively harder than P2P: a single remittance often spans multiple pages AND must remain bound to the matching check image. Numbers [PENDING-MEASUREMENT].

3. Methodology

3.1 Data-status representation

Eight-row legend per framework §0.4 (same Correct/Incorrect/Missed/ Spurious semantics as split-P2P §3.1).

3.2 Sample design

n = [PLACEHOLDER] bundles per dim. Two-level annotation: (1) split into logical sub-docs, (2) for each sub-doc, which check image is bound to it (the remit↔check pairing). κ on both.

3.3 Metrics

  • Boundary-F1.
  • Span-F1.
  • Page-attribution accuracy.
  • Full-bundle exact-match accuracy.
  • Remit↔check pairing F1 (O2C-specific; precision/recall of the

binding edges).

  • 95% bootstrap CI + n.

3.4 Scoring rubric

Same as split-P2P §3.4 plus: a remittance with the wrong check binding is Incorrect at the pairing level even if its span was Correct.

3.5 Evaluation controls

3.6 Independence and disclosure

Hyperbots-published; affiliation declared.

3.7 Limitations

  • Lockbox bundle data is bank-restricted; public-bench tier will use

synthetic-but-realistic lockbox TIFFs; partner-bench tier requires partner consent.

  • Check images on the back of a remittance (duplex scan) is a

policy-pinned strata; documented in PINS.txt.

3.8 Where this primitive sits in the IDP pipeline

split stage. Downstream: classify-O2C labels each span; extract-O2C parses open-invoice list and applied amounts; match-O2C does the cash application.
