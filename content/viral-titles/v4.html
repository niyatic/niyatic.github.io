Honesty discipline in v4

  1. Win where the data says win. Parse-intent +33% vs GPT-4.1 is the cleanest anchored relative win we have. Lead with it.
  2. Lose where the data says lose. OCR edit-distance: HyperAPI PaddleOCR OURS is 3× higher error than GPT-4o. We publish that with the win framing — credibility comes from owning the losses.
  3. "20% better than OpenAI" is too imprecise. Anchored claims are "+25.2% vs Gemini 2.5 Flash" or "+33.0% vs GPT-4.1" — the actual numbers are stronger and more defensible.
  4. The Hyperbots parse-intent cell is ATTESTED, not factory-reproduced. That's a known caveat — if the factory re-run produces different numbers, the relative claim moves. Plan: re-run hyperapi-parse-intent through the factory harness to convert to factory_reproduced: true before C-01 publishes.
  5. §C.4 placeholders are NOT secretly fabricated. They literally say [+X%] — they're the headlines we'd write IF the measurement lands. Conversion path: §C.4 → §C.1/§C.2/§C.3 after the measurement runs.
