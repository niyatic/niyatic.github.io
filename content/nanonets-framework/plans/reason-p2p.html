Reason (P2P) — deep-study plan

1. Executive summary (TEMPLATE — numbers placeholder)

Reason is the exception-routing primitive: given the output of parse/classify/extract/augment/validate/match, and given a population of held / unresolved P2P documents, decide: approve / auto-correct-and-approve / route-to: <role> / request-info: <ask> / reject, with a justification trace citing the fields, rules and external lookups that drove the decision. Hardest primitive because ground truth is itself a distribution (humans disagree). Numbers [PENDING-MEASUREMENT].

2. Scope & coverage

  • Workload. Held / exception P2P documents from validate-P2P and

match-P2P stages: tolerance excursions, missing GRN, partial-fulfilment, vendor-onboarding gaps, duplicate suspects, sanctions hits, tax excursions, multi-rule excursions.

  • Platforms. Hyperbots reason (or higher-order pipeline) + ≥1

competitor that exposes a comparable decision surface (named in §8).

  • Coverage dims. 7 of 14.
  • Test surface.
  • n = [PLACEHOLDER] exception cases per dim.
  • Stratification: exception type, dollar tier, complexity tier,

customer-policy band.

  • Three independent human reviewers per case (not two) — ground

truth is recorded as the distribution over decisions, not the single "right" answer. Cases where 3/3 humans agree are the high-confidence GT; 2/3 agreement is medium-confidence; full-disagreement cases are explicitly excluded from headline metrics and reported separately.

3. Methodology

3.1 Data-status representation

Eight-row legend. Decision-level: per case, Correct = system decision matches the modal human decision; Incorrect = differs but falls within the human-disagreement envelope; Off-distribution = a new category specific to reason — system picks an option no human picked. Off-distribution decisions are flagged separately and never counted as Correct.

3.2 Sample design

n = [PLACEHOLDER] cases per dim. Three reviewers per case; distribution recorded; modal-decision Cohen's κ-equivalent computed (Fleiss' κ for >2 raters). Macro-average across strata.

3.3 Metrics

  • Decision agreement vs modal human = primary metric.
  • **Decision agreement vs any human** = secondary (a system is

forgivable if it picks a non-modal-but-legitimate answer).

  • Off-distribution rate = fraction of decisions humans never

picked.

  • Justification-trace correctness = fraction of cases where the

cited fields/rules/lookups actually drove the human modal decision.

  • Hallucination rate = fraction of cases where the trace cites a

field/rule/lookup that does not exist in the document or master.

  • Calibration (D9) = reliability of the system's stated decision

confidence vs decision-correct-vs-modal.

  • D12 STP = fraction of cases the system can auto-resolve

(decision ∈ {approve, auto-correct-and-approve} with confidence ≥ pinned threshold) AND the auto-resolution would be ratified by the modal human.

  • 95% bootstrap CI + n. Distributions reported, not just point

estimates.

3.4 Scoring rubric

  • Decision options pinned in PINS.txt: `{approve,

auto-correct-and-approve, route-to-{AP_lead, controller, vendor}, request-info-{vendor, GRN-team}, reject}`.

  • Hallucination is a binary on the trace: any non-existent citation =

Hallucination on that case.

  • Off-distribution: any decision option not present in the human

decision distribution = Off-distribution.

3.6 Independence and disclosure

Hyperbots-published; affiliation declared. Special note (realqa-doc- ai-quality): when ground truth is a distribution, the report MUST publish the distribution shape (e.g. modal-decision share, 2-of-3 agreement share, Fleiss κ) alongside any "agreement" number; a single %-correct number is not enough. The methodology refuses to collapse distributions to a single value.

3.7 Limitations

  • GT is fuzzy. Cases with full human disagreement are excluded from

headline metrics; their share is reported as a methodology integrity indicator (high disagreement → harder/ambiguous corpus).

  • Justification-trace correctness requires human post-hoc

annotation of which fields/rules a human used; partly subjective.

  • Hallucination detection can only flag what the trace cites; a

system that gives a correct decision with no citation gets credit for the decision but [PLACEHOLDER] on traceability.

3.8 Where this primitive sits in the IDP pipeline

The reason stage, end of the pipeline (after validate / match). Output drives the human-in-the-loop queue and the auto-execute path.
