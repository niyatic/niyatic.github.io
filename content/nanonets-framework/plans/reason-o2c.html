Reason for O2C decides exceptions from validate-O2C / match-O2C: unidentified-receipt, partial-pay-with-no-deduction-code, duplicate- remit suspect, customer-over-credit-limit, dispute-eligibility, deduction-code-authority excursion, multi-currency-residual, and cross-tenant tranche reconciliation. Output = decision + justification trace. Ground truth is distributional. Numbers [PENDING-MEASUREMENT].

2. Scope & coverage

  • Workload. Exception payments / statements / remittances from

validate-O2C and match-O2C upstream.

  • Platforms. Hyperbots reason + ≥1 competitor.
  • Coverage dims. 7 of 14.
  • Test surface.
  • n = [PLACEHOLDER] exception cases per dim.
  • Stratification: exception type, dollar tier, customer-credit-tier.
  • Three reviewers per case; distribution recorded; Fleiss κ.

3. Methodology

3.1 Data-status representation

Eight-row legend + Off-distribution per reason-P2P §3.1.

3.2 Sample design

n = [PLACEHOLDER] per dim; three-reviewer GT pool; distribution reported.

3.3 Metrics

  • Decision agreement vs modal human (primary).
  • Decision agreement vs any human (secondary).
  • Off-distribution rate.
  • Justification-trace correctness.
  • Hallucination rate.
  • D9 calibration.
  • D12 STP — share of cases the system auto-resolves correctly per modal

human.

  • 95% bootstrap CI + n; distribution publication required.

3.4 Scoring rubric

  • Decision options pinned in PINS.txt: `{apply-as-on-account, apply-

with-deduction-code-X, route-to-{credit, collections, dispute}, request-info-{customer, AR}, reject-suspect-duplicate}`.

  • Hallucination = trace cites non-existent customer / invoice / code.
  • Off-distribution = decision option no reviewer picked.

3.5 Evaluation controls

Frozen sha256. Rerunnable command:


run --study reason-o2c-v0 \

3.6 Independence and disclosure

Hyperbots-published; affiliation declared. realqa-doc-ai-quality applies: distributions published alongside agreement %.

3.7 Limitations

  • Three-reviewer pool is expensive; tail strata may have lower n.
  • Dispute-eligibility decisions are deeply customer-policy-bound; the

bench uses a pinned canonical policy.
