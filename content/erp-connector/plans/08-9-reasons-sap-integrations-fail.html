The 9 reasons SAP integrations fail (and how the agent handles each) — plan

1. Headline + hook

Hook: "SAP integrations fail in 9 ways. Not 50. Not 200. Nine. They look different because the symptoms differ (a posting goes to the wrong company code, a foreign-currency invoice posts in the wrong sign, a batch hits the RFC RPS ceiling and silently retries past the closing window), but the root causes cluster. Here is the list, and here is what the connector framework's descriptor encodes for each."

Listicle-deep — short headers, dense bodies. Each of the 9 is either source-backed (from connector-framework/ or the erp-sap-s4hana-connector skill body) or tagged [PLACEHOLDER — depth claim needs source].

3.2 Depth — the 9 (each backed or [PLACEHOLDER])

  1. Wrong company code (BUKRS). Descriptor: master_key_resolver

keyed on tenant + BUKRS; cross-BUKRS posts dead-letter.

  1. Wrong fiscal year (GJAHR). Descriptor: posting-date → fiscal-year

mapping per company-code's calendar.

  1. FX-sign inversion on credit memos. Descriptor: explicit sign

convention; apply_sign is representation-only, magnitude preserved.

  1. RFC / OData RPS ceiling. Descriptor: RPM budget,

Retry-After honored, bounded retry → dead-letter.

  1. invalid_grant token churn. Secrets boundary: hard-stop on

invalid_grant, not retry; rotation honors the cross-worker write lock.

  1. Tax-code mismatch. [PLACEHOLDER — depth claim needs source from

skill body.]

  1. Vendor master mismatch. master_key_resolver lambda against the

tenant's Lifnr / BusinessPartner table.

  1. Posting-period closed. dead_letter with raw response;

the closed-period error class lands cleanly, not as a generic failure.

  1. Idempotency on retry. BusinessKey(["BUKRS", "BELNR", "GJAHR"])

lines up with SAP's own uniqueness; retries are skipped_dup.

(If the audit cuts or merges any of the 9, update the headline number; never inflate.)
